A. Routing & Cascades · 23

Route or escalate each query/turn among models.
FrugalGPT (Chen et al. 2023)
A learned LLM cascade routes each query from the cheapest model upward, using a quality scorer to decide when the current answer is good enough, matching GPT-4 at a fraction of the cost.
M003TMLR
RouteLLM (Ong et al. 2024)
Router models trained on human preference data dynamically pick a strong or weak LLM per query, cutting cost >2x at matched quality and transferring across model pairs.
M004ICLR 2025
Hybrid LLM (Ding et al. 2024)
A router assigns queries to a small (edge) or large (cloud) model based on predicted difficulty and a tunable target quality, cutting large-model calls without quality loss.
M026ICLR 2024
AutoMix (Madaan/Aggarwal et al. 2023)
A small LM few-shot self-verifies its own answer, and a POMDP meta-verifier decides whether to escalate to a larger model based on that noisy confidence signal.
M027NeurIPS 2024
RouterBench (Hu et al. 2024)
The first standardized benchmark for multi-LLM routers, evaluating them on the dollar-cost vs quality frontier using a large corpus of pre-generated inference outcomes.
M028arXiv 2024
Zooter (Lu et al. 2023, NAACL 2024)
Zooter distills rewards onto training queries to train a lightweight router that sends each query to the most expert LLM, avoiding the cost of running a reward model at inference.
M029NAACL 2024
RouterDC (Chen et al. 2024, NeurIPS 2024)
A query-based router trained with dual contrastive losses (sample-LLM and sample-sample) assembles frozen off-the-shelf LLMs, beating the best single model and prior routers.
M030NeurIPS 2024
GraphRouter (Feng et al. 2024, ICLR 2025)
GraphRouter builds an inductive heterogeneous task-query-LLM graph and predicts each response's effect and cost via edge prediction, generalizing to new LLMs without retraining.
M031ICLR 2025
MetaLLM (Nguyen et al. 2024)
MetaLLM frames per-query LLM selection as a multi-armed bandit that optimizes an accuracy-cost reward, choosing the best model without first querying the LLMs.
M032arXiv 2024
Arch-Router (Tran et al. 2025)
A compact 1.5B router maps queries to user-defined domain/action preferences, decoupling routing policy from model assignment so new models can be added without retraining.
M033arXiv 2025
Agreement-Based Cascading (Kolawole et al. 2024, TMLR)
Each cascade level runs a small ensemble and uses intra-level agreement as the data-dependent signal for whether to defer, beating the best single model on both cost and accuracy.
M034TMLR
Gatekeeper / confidence tuning (Rabanser et al. 2025, ICML workshop)
A calibration loss fine-tunes the small cascade model to be confident when it is correct and to defer when likely wrong, improving deferral quality with no architectural changes.
M035ICML 2025 workshop (TTODLer-FM)
SeqRoute (Xu et al. 2026)
SeqRoute makes budget-aware LLM routing sequential, framing a multi-turn session as a finite-horizon MDP solved with offline RL so the router learns 'delayed gratification' and avoids spending its whole budget early.
M121arXiv 2026
LLMRouterBench (Li et al. 2026)
A massive unified routing benchmark (400K+ instances, 21 datasets, 33 models, 10 baselines) whose headline finding is that many recent and commercial routers fail to reliably beat a simple baseline, and the router's backbone embedding barely matters.
M122arXiv 2026
LLM Router: Rethinking Routing with Prefill Activations (Varshney et al. 2026)
Routes queries using a model's internal prefill activations instead of surface query embeddings, using Encoder-Target Decoupling so open-weight encoders can predict the correctness of closed-source models.
M123arXiv 2026
Switchcraft (Agarwal et al. 2026)
A router built specifically for agentic tool/function calling (not chat): a DistilBERT classifier picks the cheapest correct model inline under a latency budget, and shows bigger models don't reliably win at tool use.
M124arXiv 2026
UCCI: Calibrated Uncertainty for Cost-Optimal Cascade Routing (Kotte 2026)
A calibrated cascade router maps token-margin uncertainty to a per-query error probability via isotonic regression, then picks the escalation threshold by constrained cost minimization.
M125arXiv 2026
Inter-Cascade / From Deferral to Learning (Wu et al. 2025-2026)
Turns the strong model in a cascade from a one-off helper into a long-term teacher: solutions to deferred queries become reusable strategies retrieved into the weak model's context, an online in-context distillation that cuts strong-model calls.
M126arXiv 2025/2026 (under review)
TwinRouterBench (Yang et al. 2026)
A step-level routing benchmark for long-horizon agentic apps with a static deterministic track (970 router-visible prefixes) and a live dynamic track running routers on SWE-bench Verified to measure real task resolution and API spend.
M127arXiv 2026
LLM Routing with Dueling Feedback (Chiang et al. 2025)
Casts routing as contextual dueling bandits that learn from pairwise preference feedback rather than absolute scores, with calibrated model embeddings and Feel-Good Thompson Sampling for lower regret.
M128arXiv 2025
Dynamic Model Routing and Cascading: A Survey (Moslem & Kelleher 2026)
The first dedicated 2026 survey of cross-model LLM routing and cascading (distinct from within-model MoE), taxonomizing methods by when decisions are made, what information they use, and how they are computed.
M129arXiv 2026
BudgetMem / Query-Aware Budget-Tier Routing (Zhang et al. 2026)
A lightweight RL-trained router performs query-aware budget-tier routing across agent-memory modules, trading task performance against memory-construction cost over Low/Mid/High tiers.
M130ICML'26
Reward-Based Online LLM Routing via NeuralUCB (Tsai & Tran 2026)
Frames cost-aware LLM routing as a contextual bandit solved with NeuralUCB, learning online from a utility reward to undercut a max-quality reference on cost while staying competitive on reward.

B. Ensembling & Aggregation · 20

Fuse, rank, or vote many outputs into one.
Mixture-of-Agents (Wang et al. 2024)
Mixture-of-Agents (MoA) stacks multiple LLM agents in layers, where each agent conditions on all outputs from the previous layer, and an open-model ensemble surpasses GPT-4 Omni on AlpacaEval 2.0.
M001arXiv 2024 (COLM'24)
LLM-Blender (Jiang et al. 2023)
LLM-Blender ensembles open-source LLMs by first ranking candidate outputs pairwise (PairRanker) and then generatively fusing the top-k into an improved answer (GenFuser).
M009ACL 2023
Self-Consistency (Wang et al. 2022)
Self-consistency samples multiple diverse chain-of-thought reasoning paths from a single model and returns the majority-voted answer, a homogeneous single-model ensemble.
M017ICLR 2023
More Agents Is All You Need / Agent Forest (Li et al. 2024)
A simple sampling-and-voting method ('Agent Forest') shows LLM performance scales with the number of instantiated agents, and this gain is orthogonal to other enhancement techniques.
M018TMLR 2024
Self-MoA / Rethinking Mixture-of-Agents (Li et al. 2025)
A negative result for mixing: aggregating repeated samples from the single best-performing model (Self-MoA) beats standard Mixture-of-Agents that mixes different LLMs.
M036arXiv 2025
DeePEn (Huang et al. 2024)
DeePEn is a training-free ensemble that fuses heterogeneous LLMs' per-step probability distributions by mapping them into a shared 'relative representation' space that resolves vocabulary mismatch.
M037NeurIPS 2024
PackLLM (Mavromatis et al. 2024)
PackLLM ('Pack of LLMs') fuses arbitrary user-specified LLMs at test time by weighting each model according to how well it minimizes perplexity on the input prompt, with no trained fusion module.
M038arXiv 2024
FuseLLM / Knowledge Fusion of LLMs (Wan et al. 2024)
FuseLLM combines the capabilities of multiple pre-trained source LLMs into a single target model by distilling their generative probability distributions via lightweight continual training, even across differing architectures.
M039ICLR 2024
Are More LLM Calls All You Need? (Chen et al. 2024)
A scaling-laws study showing that majority-vote aggregation (Vote and Filter-Vote) is non-monotone in the number of LM calls: accuracy first rises then falls as tasks mix easy and hard queries.
M040NeurIPS 2024
LLM Ensemble Survey (Chen et al. 2025)
The first systematic survey of LLM ensembling, organizing methods by timing into ensemble-before-, during-, and after-inference categories with a curated resource repository.
M041arXiv 2025 (ACL'25 findings)
Should we be going MAD? (Smit et al. 2023)
A negative benchmarking study finding that multi-agent debate (MAD) does not reliably beat self-consistency or multi-path ensembling and is highly sensitive to hyperparameters.
M042ICML 2024
Stop Overvaluing Multi-Agent Debate / Heter-MAD (Wang et al. 2025)
A position/evaluation paper arguing multi-agent debate is overvalued: across 5 MAD methods x 9 benchmarks x 4 models it often fails to beat CoT/self-consistency at far higher compute, but model heterogeneity (Heter-MAD) recovers gains.
M043arXiv 2025
Self-Consistent Mixture of Agents / Trace-Level Synthesis (Fadnavis et al. 2026)
Argues the unit of aggregation should be the reasoning TRACE, not the final answer: an aggregator reading full traces recovers correct solutions even under unanimous agreement (the 'aggregation paradox'), and single-model perturbation traces beat heterogeneous model pools.
M117arXiv 2026
Mixture of Complementary Agents (Zhang et al. 2026)
Reframes proposer selection for the N-to-1 MoA summarizer as a combinatorial feature-selection problem, where a model's value is its complementarity with others, solved via greedy selection gauged on a small labeled set.
M118arXiv 2026
Structured Message Passing for Multi-LLM Aggregation (Mehrabi et al. 2026)
Aggregates domain-specialized LLMs at the semantic level via a bipartite factor graph plus error-recovery-inspired message passing, using asymmetric damping to shield strong 'anchor' models from weak members, with no extra LLM calls during refinement.
M119arXiv 2026
SMoA — Sparse Mixture-of-Agents (Li et al. 2024)
SMoA applies sparse mixture-of-experts principles to Mixture-of-Agents, sparsifying inter-agent information flow via Response Selection and Early Stopping plus distinct role descriptions, improving the performance-efficiency balance of dense MoA.
M120arXiv 2024
Beyond Majority Voting (Ai et al. 2026)
A training-free aggregation framework that beats equal-weight majority voting by using both first- and second-order information via two algorithms, Optimal Weight and Inverse Surprising Popularity, with theoretical guarantees.
M132ICML'26
LLMBoost (Chen et al. 2025)
A boosting-style ensemble fine-tuning framework that fuses predecessor models' hidden states via cross-model attention under an error-suppression chain-training objective, with near-parallel layer-pipelined inference, going beyond black-box output voting.
M133arXiv 2025
Attention-MoA (Wen et al. 2026)
A Mixture-of-Agents variant adding Inter-Agent Semantic Attention plus inter-layer residual synthesis and adaptive early stopping to curb information loss in deep MoA layers, reaching 91.15% LC win-rate on AlpacaEval 2.0 and 8.83 on MT-Bench with small open models.
M134arXiv 2026
Co-Failure Ceiling: When Does Combining Models Help? (Chen 2026)
A critique across 67 frontier models arguing gains from routing, voting, cascades, and MoA are capped by 'beta', the rate at which all models fail on the same query; average pairwise error correlation cannot identify beta, and combining rarely beats the single best model without a strong query-level routing signal.
M135arXiv 2026

C. Role-Specialized Orchestration · 19

Planner/worker/subagent teams coordinated by a manager or pipeline.
AutoGen (Wu et al. 2023)
AutoGen is an open-source framework of customizable, conversable agents that solve tasks by messaging one another, mixing LLMs, tools, and human input.
M010arXiv 2023 (later COLM 2024 workshop / widely cited)
MetaGPT (Hong et al. 2023)
MetaGPT encodes human Standardized Operating Procedures (SOPs) into prompt sequences and an assembly-line role assignment to reduce cascading hallucination in LLM agent teams.
M011ICLR 2024 (oral)
CAMEL (Li et al. 2023)
CAMEL uses role-playing with 'inception prompting' to let two communicative agents autonomously cooperate on a task, also generating conversational data for studying agent behavior.
M012NeurIPS 2023
Anthropic Multi-Agent Research System (2025)
An orchestrator-worker research system where a Claude Opus 4 lead agent spawns parallel Claude Sonnet 4 subagents that each search independently and return filtered findings for synthesis.
M013Anthropic engineering blog (2025)
ChatDev (Qian et al. 2023/ACL 2024)
ChatDev is a chat-powered software-development framework where role-specialized LLM agents (CEO/CTO/programmer/tester) collaborate along a 'chat chain' with communicative dehallucination to build software end-to-end.
M050ACL 2024
AgentVerse (Chen et al. 2023/ICLR 2024)
AgentVerse dynamically composes an expert-agent group via a four-stage recruit/decide/act/evaluate loop, achieving a 'greater-than-the-sum-of-its-parts' system that outperforms a single agent and shows emergent social behaviors.
M051ICLR 2024
MacNet / Scaling LLM Multi-Agent Collaboration (Qian et al. 2024)
MacNet organizes LLM agents as directed acyclic graphs, scales to 1000+ collaborating agents, and identifies a 'collaborative scaling law' of logistic quality growth, with small-world/irregular topologies performing best.
M052ICLR 2025
DyLAN (Liu et al. 2023)
DyLAN is a Dynamic LLM-Powered Agent Network that selects a task-specific team using an unsupervised Agent Importance Score and lets agents collaborate dynamically, deactivating weak contributors.
M053COLM 2024
Magentic-One (Fourney et al./Microsoft 2024)
Magentic-One is a generalist manager-worker multi-agent system where an Orchestrator with task/progress ledgers directs specialized Coder, Executor, FileSurfer, and WebSurfer agents, reaching competitive results on GAIA, AssistantBench, and WebArena.
M054arXiv 2024 (Microsoft; built on AutoGen)
OpenAI Swarm / Agents SDK (2024-2025)
OpenAI Swarm is a minimal orchestration library built on two primitives—routines (an agent = instructions + tools) and handoffs (a function that returns another agent)—later superseded by the production Agents SDK.
M055OpenAI (GitHub, Oct 2024) / Agents SDK (Mar 2025)
LangGraph vs CrewAI (framework references)
Two dominant production orchestration idioms: LangGraph models agents as an explicit state-machine graph (typed shared state, checkpointing, cycles), while CrewAI models top-down role-based crews (sequential or hierarchical processes).
M056Framework documentation (LangChain / CrewAI)
AOrchestra: Automating Sub-Agent Creation for Agentic Orchestration (Ruan et al. 2026)
AOrchestra has a central orchestrator that spawns task-specific sub-agents on the fly from a unified four-tuple (Instruction, Context, Tools, Model), replacing manually predefined static roles.
M151ICML 2026
AdaptOrch: Task-Adaptive Multi-Agent Orchestration in the Era of LLM Performance Convergence (Yu 2026)
AdaptOrch argues that as provider LLMs converge on benchmarks the orchestration topology, not model choice, dominates, and dynamically routes each task DAG to a parallel/sequential/hierarchical/hybrid topology.
M152arXiv 2026 (preprint)
Multi-Agent Teams Hold Experts Back (Pappu et al. 2026)
A negative result: self-organizing LLM teams consistently fail to match their best individual expert (up to 41.1% loss on ML benchmarks) even when told which agent is the expert; the bottleneck is expert leveraging, not identification.
M153ICML 2026
AutoScientists: Self-Organizing Agent Teams for Long-Running Scientific Experimentation (Gao, Fang, Zitnik 2026)
AutoScientists is a decentralized multi-agent system (no central planner) where agents read a shared experimental state, self-organize into teams around promising hypotheses, critique proposals before spending compute, and share wins and failures.
M154arXiv 2026 (preprint)
HERA / Experience as a Compass: Multi-agent RAG with Evolving Orchestration (Li & Ramakrishnan 2026)
HERA is a hierarchical multi-agent RAG framework that jointly evolves query-specific agent topologies (reward-guided sampling) and role-specific prompts (Role-Aware Prompt Evolution) without gradient training, reporting emergent self-organization into compact high-utility networks.
M155arXiv 2026 (preprint)
The Ringelmann Effect in Multi-Agent LLM Systems: A Scaling Law for Effective Team Size (Bertalanic & Fortuna 2026)
Derives a two-parameter scaling law R(N)=N_eff/N that sorts agent configs into hard-ceiling/sublinear/linear regimes, and shows negatively that 30 dense debating agents add no more diversity than one while a noise placebo matches self-correction gains; only heterogeneous teams escape the ceiling.
M156arXiv 2026 (preprint)
Understanding Agent Scaling in LLM-Based Multi-Agent Systems via Diversity (2026)
An information-theoretic account of why adding agents saturates: homogeneous outputs are correlated so gains cap at task uncertainty, while heterogeneous agents add complementary channels; empirically 2 diverse agents match or exceed 16 homogeneous ones.
M157arXiv 2026 (preprint)
Reinforcement Learning for LLM-based Multi-Agent Systems through Orchestration Traces (Zhang 2026)
A survey/position paper organizing RL for multi-agent orchestration around 'orchestration traces'—temporal interaction graphs of spawn/delegate/communicate/tool-use/aggregate/stop events—along reward design, credit signals, and five orchestration sub-decisions; it flags that no existing RL method trains the stopping decision.
M165arXiv 2026 (survey/position, preprint)

D. Debate / Critique / Refine · 17

Instances exchange and revise reasoning over rounds.
Multiagent Debate (Du et al. 2023)
Multiple LLM instances independently propose answers and then debate over several rounds until they converge on a shared final answer, improving factuality and reasoning without any training.
M002ICML 2024
Self-Refine (Madaan et al. 2023)
A single LLM plays generator, feedback-provider, and refiner, iteratively critiquing and improving its own output at test time with no extra training.
M014NeurIPS 2023
Reflexion (Shinn et al. 2023)
Language agents improve across trials by verbally reflecting on task feedback and storing those reflections in an episodic memory buffer, reinforcing behavior without any weight updates.
M015NeurIPS 2023
ReConcile (Chen et al. 2023)
A round-table conference of diverse LLM agents discusses over multiple rounds and uses confidence-weighted voting to reach a stronger consensus answer.
M022ACL 2024
LLMs Cannot Self-Correct Reasoning Yet (Huang et al. 2023, DeepMind)
A critical study showing that intrinsic self-correction (no external feedback) does not reliably improve reasoning and can even degrade it, and that multi-agent debate does not beat self-consistency at a matched sample budget.
M059ICLR 2024
AI Safety via Debate (Irving, Christiano, Amodei 2018, OpenAI)
A foundational alignment proposal: two agents debate a question and a weaker human judge picks the more truthful/useful side, with the adversarial structure making lying harder than exposing a lie.
M060arXiv 2018 (OpenAI)
Debating with More Persuasive LLMs Leads to More Truthful Answers (Khan et al. 2024)
In judge-mediated debate for scalable oversight, optimizing debaters to be more persuasive raises a non-expert judge's accuracy, and adversarial debate beats non-adversarial consultancy and naive baselines.
M061ICML 2024
On Scalable Oversight with Weak LLMs Judging Strong LLMs (Kenton et al. 2024, DeepMind)
A broader test of debate as oversight with weak judges over strong debaters: debate beats single-agent consultancy and helps judge accuracy, but the benefit over direct QA is task-dependent and the persuasiveness effect is smaller than earlier work suggested.
M062NeurIPS 2024
Improving Multi-Agent Debate with Sparse Communication Topology (Li et al. 2024)
Default multi-agent debate is all-to-all; using a sparse communication graph achieves comparable or better accuracy while significantly cutting token/compute cost, on both GPT and Mistral models.
M063arXiv 2024
ChatEval (Chan et al. 2023)
A multi-agent 'referee team' of LLMs debates to evaluate generated text, improving LLM-as-judge alignment with human judgment beyond single-agent prompting.
M064arXiv 2023
The Cost of Consensus: Isolated Self-Correction Prevails Over Unguided Homogeneous Multi-Agent Debate (Bertalanic & Fortuna 2026)
A NEGATIVE controlled study: for 7-8B homogeneous agent teams, unguided peer debate costs 2.1-3.4x more tokens for equal or worse accuracy than isolated self-correction, and it identifies three knowledge-destruction pathways.
M158ACM Conference on AI and Agentic Systems 2026
Demystifying Multi-Agent Debate: The Role of Confidence and Diversity (2026)
Proves that with homogeneous agents and uniform belief updates, debate merely preserves expected correctness and cannot reliably beat majority vote; two lightweight fixes (diversity-aware initialization + calibrated-confidence-modulated debate) recover gains across 6 QA benchmarks.
M159arXiv 2026
Heterogeneous LLM Debate Under Adversarial Peers (Nilayam et al. 2026)
Cross-model evidence that a diverse peer is double-edged: an honest heterogeneous peer sharply cuts harmful revisions (Llama-3.1-70B on MATH-hard: 89%->35%), but an adversarial one reverses it (->90%); heterogeneity is both an attack surface and, given an existing adversary, a defense.
M160arXiv 2026
Detection Without Correction: A Two-Parameter Decomposition of Multi-Stage LLM Pipelines (Nilayam et al. 2026)
A NEGATIVE-mechanism paper that reframes a downstream agent's response as detection (should upstream content be trusted?) plus conditional generation, unifying self-correction degradation and non-replication of debate gains as one failure mode where conditional miscorrection dominates at 53-94%.
M161arXiv 2026
Not All Flips Are Conformity: Decomposing Stance Convergence in Multi-Agent LLM Debate (Hao et al. 2026)
A NEGATIVE analysis showing the standard 'answer flip rate' conflates spontaneous instability, stance-induced conformity, and reasoning-induced persuasion; strict conformity (29%) is mostly harmful (57-77% correct-to-wrong) and even empty reasoning drives 20-39% error adoption, but harmful conformity is predictable at Round 0 (AUC 0.79).
M162arXiv 2026
Diversity Collapse in Multi-Agent LLM Systems (Chen et al. 2026)
A NEGATIVE result for open-ended ideation: multi-agent collaboration reduces idea diversity because of interaction structure, not model limits; documents a compute-efficiency paradox, authority-driven diversity suppression, and dense-topology premature convergence.
M163ACL 2026 Findings
Epistemic Gain, Aleatoric Cost: Uncertainty Decomposition in Multi-Agent Debate (Qiao et al. 2026)
A Bayesian framework splitting debate predictive uncertainty into epistemic 'gain' vs aleatoric 'cost', showing effective debate needs high epistemic gain under controlled aleatoric cost, and builds an uncertainty-guided MARL method for math reasoning.
M164ICML 2026

E. Verifier-Gated & LLM-as-Judge · 19

One model scores, verifies, or gates another's output.
LLM-as-a-Judge / MT-Bench & Chatbot Arena (Zheng et al. 2023)
Strong LLM judges approximate human preference at scale and exhibit position, verbosity, and self-enhancement biases.
M016NeurIPS 2023 Datasets & Benchmarks Track
Let's Verify Step by Step / PRM800K (Lightman et al. 2023)
A process reward model scoring each reasoning step is a stronger verifier for best-of-N selection than an outcome reward model.
M070arXiv preprint (OpenAI); ICLR 2024
Training Verifiers to Solve Math Word Problems (Cobbe et al. 2021, GSM8K)
Best-of-N selection by a trained verifier (outcome reward model) beats plain finetuning and scales better with data than the generator.
M071arXiv preprint (OpenAI)
Generative Verifiers / GenRM (Zhang et al. 2024)
Training the verifier with next-token prediction (verify-by-generating with chain-of-thought) beats discriminative reward models and LLM-as-judge on best-of-N.
M072ICLR 2025
Self-Taught Evaluators (Wang et al. 2024)
An iterative loop trains an LLM-as-judge on synthetic contrastive outputs with no human preference labels, rivaling reward models trained on human data.
M073arXiv preprint (Meta FAIR)
CriticGPT / LLM Critics Help Catch LLM Bugs (McAleese et al., OpenAI 2024)
An RLHF-trained critic writes natural-language critiques of code that trainers prefer over human critiques and that find more bugs, with human+critic teams hallucinating less than the critic alone.
M074arXiv preprint (OpenAI)
Replacing Judges with Juries / Panel of LLM evaluators (PoLL) (Verga et al. 2024)
A panel of several smaller judges from disjoint model families correlates better with humans, is far cheaper, and has less intra-model bias than one large judge.
M075arXiv preprint (Cohere)
Large Language Models are not Fair Evaluators (Wang et al. 2023)
LLM-as-judge verdicts are strongly position-biased and can be flipped by reordering candidates; multiple-evidence and balanced-position calibration mitigate it.
M076ACL 2024
JudgeBench (Tan et al. 2024)
On objectively-checkable but challenging response pairs, even strong LLM judges perform only slightly above random, exposing reliability limits.
M077ICLR 2025
Scaling LLM Test-Time Compute Optimally (Snell et al. 2024)
Compute-optimal PRM-verifier search plus sequential revisions lets a small model beat a 14x larger one under matched FLOPs, gating quality via a verifier rather than parameters.
M078arXiv preprint (UC Berkeley / Google DeepMind); ICLR 2025
Reliability without Validity (Norman et al. 2026)
The largest LLM-as-judge audit to date shows exact-match agreement inflates apparent judge quality relative to chance-corrected agreement, so a judge can be reliable yet invalid.
M181arXiv preprint (no venue confirmed)
Judging the Judges (Soumik 2026, TMLR)
A systematic comparison of debiasing strategies across judges and provider families finds a debiased mid-tier judge can beat frontier judges at ~15x lower cost, and that style bias dominates position bias.
M182TMLR 2026
RewardBench 2 (Malik et al. 2026, ICLR'26)
A harder, multi-skill, accuracy-based reward-model benchmark on newly-sourced human prompts where models score ~20 points lower than RewardBench-1 yet correlate strongly with downstream best-of-N and PPO performance.
M183ICLR 2026
Agent-as-a-Judge Survey (You et al. 2026)
The first comprehensive survey of the shift from LLM-as-Judge to Agent-as-Judge, where agentic judges add planning, tool-augmented verification, multi-agent collaboration, and persistent memory to overcome single-pass judge biases.
M184arXiv preprint (Jan 2026)
AJ-Bench (Shi et al. 2026, ACL'26 Findings)
An environment-aware benchmark for Agent-as-a-Judge across search, data, and GUI domains that tests a judge's information acquisition, state verification, and process verification through tool and environment interaction.
M185ACL 2026 Findings
DataPRM (Qiu et al. 2026, KDD'26)
An environment-aware generative process reward model that acts as an active verifier interacting with the environment to catch silent errors, using reflection-aware ternary rewards for agentic data analysis.
M186KDD 2026
AgentV-RL / Agentic Verifier (Zhang et al. 2026, ACL'26)
Reframes reward modeling as a multi-turn, tool-augmented deliberative process with forward and backward verification agents, RL-trained so the verifier itself scales at test time under both parallel and sequential test-time scaling.
M187ACL 2026
CARE: Confounder-Aware Aggregation (Zhao et al. 2026)
A judge-ensemble aggregation that models scores as true quality plus shared confounders (verbosity, style), separating signal from bias without ground-truth labels; naive majority/average voting can amplify correlated judge errors.
M188arXiv preprint
CyclicJudge (Zhu et al. 2026)
A round-robin assignment of a judge panel to scenarios that recovers the full-panel mean score exactly at single-judge cost, backed by a variance decomposition over scenario, generation, judge, and residual factors.

F. Strong-Weak Efficiency · 25

Weak model does bulk work; strong model verifies or refines.
Speculative Decoding (Leviathan et al. 2023)
A small drafter proposes tokens that the large target model verifies in parallel, giving exact-distribution speedups without retraining or architecture changes.
M005ICML 2023 (Oral)
Speculative Sampling (Chen et al. 2023, DeepMind)
A concurrent draft model proposes short continuations that the target scores in parallel, with a modified rejection-sampling step accelerating decoding losslessly.
M006arXiv 2023 (DeepMind)
EAGLE (Li et al. 2024)
EAGLE drafts at the second-to-top-layer feature level (not token level) and resolves feature uncertainty by conditioning on a one-step-shifted token sequence, raising acceptance and speedup.
M007ICML 2024
Big Little Decoder (Kim et al. 2023)
A small model decodes autoregressively while a large model is called occasionally to refine only uncertain predictions non-autoregressively, via learned fallback and rollback policies.
M008NeurIPS 2023
Medusa (Cai et al. 2024)
Extra lightweight decoding heads on a frozen backbone predict several future tokens in parallel, verified via tree attention; Medusa-1 is lossless while Medusa-2 uses typical-acceptance.
M079ICML 2024
Lookahead Decoding (Fu et al. 2024)
A draft-model-free, exact parallel decoding algorithm that uses Jacobi iteration and n-gram trajectories to break the sequential dependency of autoregressive decoding.
M080ICML 2024
SpecInfer (Miao et al. 2023)
Small speculative models propose a token tree that the LLM verifies in parallel as a tree verifier, provably preserving output quality while cutting latency.
M081ASPLOS 2024
Staged Speculative Decoding (Spector & Re 2023)
Restructures the speculative batch as a tree and adds a second speculation stage (speculating on the draft itself) to accelerate small-batch, on-device inference losslessly.
M082ICML 2023 ES-FOMO Workshop
Self-Speculative Decoding / Draft & Verify (Zhang et al. 2023)
The model drafts by selectively skipping its own intermediate layers, then verifies those tokens with the full model in one forward pass, making it draft-model-free and lossless.
M083ACL 2024
Blockwise Parallel Decoding (Stern et al. 2018)

M190ICLR 2026

G. Communication & Memory Protocols · 9

MCP is an open JSON-RPC standard, likened to a 'USB-C port for AI', that connects AI applications to external data sources, tools, and workflows through a common client-server interface.

A2A is an open peer-to-peer protocol that lets AI agents built on different frameworks and by different vendors discover one another, delegate tasks, and share results without exposing their internal memory, tools, or logic.

M096arXiv 2025 (preprint)

This paper organizes agent communication protocols into a three-layer taxonomy (communication, syntactic, semantic) inspired by Shannon-Weaver and Gricean pragmatics, and shows that current protocols are mature on transport/syntax but under-support semantic alignment such as clarification and repair.
M097arXiv 2026 (preprint)
MemGPT (Packer et al. 2023)
MemGPT applies operating-system memory-hierarchy ideas to LLMs, letting the model self-manage tiered memory - a fixed main context (like RAM) plus external storage (like disk) - via function calls, so it behaves as if it had a far larger context window.
M098arXiv 2023 (later COLM 2024); now the Letta framework

A-MEM is a Zettelkasten-inspired agentic memory in which each memory is an atomic note with LLM-generated keywords, tags, and contextual description, dynamically linked to related notes and allowed to evolve as new memories arrive - moving beyond static RAG retrieval.
M099NeurIPS 2025
ProtocolBench / ProtocolRouter (Du, Zhu et al. 2025/2026)
ProtocolBench isolates the effect of the agent communication protocol by holding LLM, prompts, and hardware constant while varying the protocol (A2A/ACP/ANP/Agora), and ProtocolRouter is a learnable per-module router that selects protocols from requirement and runtime signals.

This ICML 2026 position paper argues that collaborative agentic AI needs minimal shared standards to avoid fragmenting into incompatible ecosystems, and proposes 'Web of Agents' as a minimal architectural foundation built from four components.
M212ICML 2026 (position paper)

The Mesh Memory Protocol (MMP) is a proposed semantic-infrastructure layer that lets teams of LLM agents share, evaluate, and combine each other's cognitive state across sessions, built from four composable primitives (CAT7, SVAF, inter-agent lineage, remix).

Z. Benchmarks & Failure modes · 9

How these systems are measured — and how they fail.
Why Do Multi-Agent LLM Systems Fail? / MAST (Cemri et al. 2025)
An empirical study builds MAST, the first Multi-Agent System failure Taxonomy, cataloguing 14 failure modes in 3 categories, derived from human annotation of real MAS execution traces.
M023NeurIPS 2025 Datasets & Benchmarks (arXiv v3)
Talk Isn't Always Cheap: Understanding Failure Modes in Multi-Agent Debate (Wynn et al. 2025)
Multi-agent debate can reduce accuracy rather than improve it, because agents shift from correct to incorrect answers under peer pressure, and a weaker minority can drag down a stronger majority.

When test-time compute is normalized to equal thinking-token budgets, single-agent systems match or beat multiple multi-agent architectures on multi-hop reasoning, implying many reported MAS gains are a compute/context confound.

AgentCollabBench (2026)
A 900-task human-validated benchmark that diagnoses process-level MAS failures under a fixed protocol, isolating four behavioral risks and revealing a synthesis bottleneck specific to converging-DAG topologies.
M225arXiv 2026 (cs.CL; no formal venue listed)
When KV Cache Reuse Fails in Multi-Agent Systems (Liang et al. 2026)
KV-cache-reuse tricks that speed up generation agents break LLM judges: they weaken cross-candidate attention so the judge's selections become inconsistent with dense prefill even when task accuracy appears stable.
M226arXiv 2026 (cs.MA / cs.CL; no formal venue listed)
