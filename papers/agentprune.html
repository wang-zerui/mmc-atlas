AgentPrune / 'Cut the Crap' (Zhang et al. 2024)

M107arXiv 2410.02506verified source ↗
AgentPrune identifies and formally defines communication redundancy in LLM multi-agent systems and performs one-shot pruning on the spatial-temporal message-passing graph to remove redundant or malicious messages, yielding a token-economic, high-performing topology.

Background

LLM-agent collective intelligence often outperforms individuals thanks to carefully designed inter-agent communication topologies.

Problem

Existing multi-agent pipelines introduce substantial token overhead and economic cost, which impedes large-scale deployment.

Motivation / Analysis

If much inter-agent communication is redundant, pruning it could cut cost dramatically while preserving performance and even improving robustness to malicious messages.

Key Insight

Communication redundancy can be formally defined over a spatial-temporal message-passing graph, and a one-shot pruning of low-value edges produces a cheaper topology that keeps quality and filters adversarial content.

Challenges

Distinguishing redundant/malicious edges from useful ones, integrating pruning into diverse existing frameworks, and defending against agent-based adversarial attacks.

Method

AgentPrune builds a spatial-temporal message-passing graph over agent interactions and applies one-shot pruning (via trainable communication masks) to eliminate redundant or malicious edges; it plugs into mainstream multi-agent systems.

Experimental Setup

Extensive experiments across six benchmarks, measuring cost, token reduction, and robustness against two types of agent-based adversarial attacks.

Results

Matches state-of-the-art topologies at $5.6 cost versus $43.7, integrates into existing frameworks with 28.1%-72.8% token reduction, and defends against two adversarial-attack types with a 3.5%-10.8% performance boost.

Limitations

One-shot pruning is derived on the evaluated benchmarks; the batch note flags that the exact magnitudes were not independently audited, and generalization of the pruned topology to new tasks is not fully characterized.

Relation to the Field

An inference-time communication-graph optimizer contrasting with GPTSwarm's learned static graphs; a common baseline for later topology methods (Maestro, HieraMAS, MAGE) and complementary to Optima's training-based token savings.
