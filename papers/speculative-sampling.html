Speculative Sampling (Chen et al. 2023, DeepMind)

M006arXiv 2023 (DeepMind)verified source ↗
A concurrent draft model proposes short continuations that the target scores in parallel, with a modified rejection-sampling step accelerating decoding losslessly.

Background

Transformer decoding is dominated by the serial cost of generating one token per forward pass, which is especially expensive for very large models like 70B Chinchilla.

Problem

Speed up decoding of a large target model by producing several tokens per target call without compromising sample quality or modifying the model.

Motivation / Analysis

Scoring a short candidate continuation from a small draft model in parallel costs about the same wall-clock time as sampling a single token from the large target, so several tokens can be validated at once.

Key Insight

A modified rejection sampling scheme over draft tokens keeps the target model's output distribution exactly intact while allowing multiple accepted tokens per step.

Challenges

Ensuring the acceptance/rejection procedure preserves the target distribution up to hardware numerics, and pairing an effective draft model with the target.

Method

A smaller draft model generates candidate tokens autoregressively; the target model scores all candidates in one parallel pass; a novel modified rejection sampling step accepts a prefix and resamples on rejection.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on Chinchilla 70B in a distributed setup with a smaller draft model, on standard benchmarks including HumanEval and XSum.

Results

2-2.5x speedup on 70B Chinchilla (verified in claim_checked). The method is distribution-preserving (lossless): it preserves the target's distribution within hardware numerics with no quality loss.

Limitations

Gains depend on draft-target acceptance rates; distribution preservation holds only up to hardware numerical precision; requires a compatible faster draft model.

Relation to the Field

Concurrent and complementary to Leviathan et al.'s Speculative Decoding; both established the lossless draft-verify paradigm underpinning EAGLE, Medusa, and SpecInfer.
