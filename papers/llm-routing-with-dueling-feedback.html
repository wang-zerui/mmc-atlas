LLM Routing with Dueling Feedback (Chiang et al. 2025)

M128arXiv 2025verified source ↗
Casts routing as contextual dueling bandits that learn from pairwise preference feedback rather than absolute scores, with calibrated model embeddings and Feel-Good Thompson Sampling for lower regret.

Background

Online routers must learn which model to pick while only observing feedback on the models they actually query.

Problem

Absolute-score feedback is hard to obtain and noisy; routers need to learn from cheaper, more reliable pairwise comparisons.

Motivation / Analysis

Pairwise preference (which of two answers is better) is easier to collect than calibrated absolute quality, motivating a dueling-bandit formulation.

Key Insight

Routing fits contextual dueling bandits: learn from comparisons between models, using calibrated embeddings and an optimistic Thompson-sampling algorithm to explore efficiently.

Challenges

Producing model embeddings that transfer across categories (via Category-Calibrated Fine-Tuning) and controlling exploration/regret in the dueling setting.

Method

Routing is formulated as contextual dueling bandits; Category-Calibrated Fine-Tuning (CCFT) learns contrastive model embeddings, and Feel-Good Thompson Sampling for contextual dueling bandits (FGTS.CDB) drives online selection from pairwise feedback.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on RouterBench and MixInstruct, measuring cumulative regret and cost balance against baselines.

Results

Achieves lower cumulative regret and better cost balance on RouterBench/MixInstruct; confirms the contextual-dueling-bandit formulation, CCFT contrastive embeddings, and FGTS.CDB algorithm (verified in claim_checked); submitted 1 Oct 2025, preprint (cs.LG).

Limitations

Requires pairwise-comparison feedback; evaluated on two benchmarks; no venue claimed.

Relation to the Field

An online-bandit router in the lineage of MetaLLM (M032) and NeuralUCB routing (M131), distinguished by preference/dueling feedback rather than absolute reward.
