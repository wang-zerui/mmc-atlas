CriticGPT / LLM Critics Help Catch LLM Bugs (McAleese et al., OpenAI 2024)

M074arXiv preprint (OpenAI)verified source ↗
An RLHF-trained critic writes natural-language critiques of code that trainers prefer over human critiques and that find more bugs, with human+critic teams hallucinating less than the critic alone.

Background

RLHF relies on human trainers to evaluate model outputs, but as models tackle harder tasks like code, humans increasingly miss subtle errors, threatening the reliability of the reward signal.

Problem

Human evaluators cannot reliably catch bugs in complex model-written code, degrading the quality of RLHF feedback (a scalable-oversight problem).

Motivation / Analysis

Assisting human trainers with an AI critic that surfaces flaws could keep human oversight effective as model capability outpaces unaided human judgment.

Key Insight

A model trained with RLHF specifically to critique code can point out real bugs in natural language, and human+critic teams outperform either alone, demonstrating scalable oversight.

Challenges

Critics can hallucinate nonexistent problems or nitpick; balancing recall of real bugs against hallucinated ones is central.

Method

CriticGPT is trained with RLHF to write natural-language critiques of ChatGPT-produced code; it is evaluated on both inserted bugs and naturally-occurring bugs, and in human+critic team settings.

Experimental Setup

Human preference comparisons of critic vs human critiques, bug-detection rates versus paid code reviewers, and precision/recall trade-offs with and without human assistance.

Results

Trainers preferred CriticGPT's critiques in 63% of cases on naturally-occurring bugs, and the critic catches more bugs than paid human reviewers; human+critic teams hallucinate less than the critic alone (confirmed in abstract).

Limitations

Focused on relatively short code snippets with single bugs; critics still hallucinate and may not scale to large, distributed real-world defects.

Relation to the Field

A critique-generating verifier for scalable oversight; conceptually adjacent to Generative Verifiers and Agent-as-a-Judge, which also produce reasoned verification rather than scalar scores.
