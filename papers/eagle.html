EAGLE (Li et al. 2024)

M007ICML 2024verified source ↗
EAGLE drafts at the second-to-top-layer feature level (not token level) and resolves feature uncertainty by conditioning on a one-step-shifted token sequence, raising acceptance and speedup.

Background

Speculative sampling accelerates LLM decoding, but token-level drafters have limited acceptance rates because next-token prediction is inherently ambiguous.

Problem

Increase the acceptance rate and speedup of speculative sampling by making the drafting task easier and more accurate at low overhead.

Motivation / Analysis

The authors observe that autoregression at the feature (hidden-state) level is more regular and easier to predict than at the token level, but feature-level uncertainty still limits naive prediction.

Key Insight

Feeding the drafter a token sequence advanced by one time step removes the feature-level uncertainty, enabling accurate second-to-top-layer feature prediction with minimal added cost.

Challenges

Feature-level autoregression carries inherent uncertainty that must be resolved; the drafting head must stay lightweight while accurately predicting features.

Method

A small autoregressive head predicts the target model's second-to-top-layer features, conditioned on both prior features and a one-time-step-shifted token sequence; predicted features are mapped to token distributions and verified by the target.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on Vicuna, LLaMA2-Chat (up to 70B), and Mixtral 8x7B Instruct across dialogue, code generation, math reasoning, and instruction following.

Results

2.7x-3.5x latency speedup on LLaMA2-Chat 70B with doubled throughput (verified in claim_checked; EAGLE-3 reaches ~3-6.5x). The method maintains the target's text distribution and is distribution-preserving (lossless).

Limitations

Requires training a feature-level drafting head per target model; gains vary by task and model size.

Relation to the Field

Refines token-level speculative sampling (Leviathan, Chen) by drafting in feature space; a strong baseline that later methods like VSD and PARSE compare against and compose with.
