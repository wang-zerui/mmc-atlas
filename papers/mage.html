MAGE: Multi-Agent Comm Graph Generation via Score-Based Diffusion (Zhao et al. 2026)

M219ICML 2026 (Poster)verified source ↗
MAGE reframes multi-agent communication-graph design as an energy-minimization problem grounded in non-equilibrium thermodynamics, generating task-adaptive topologies via score-based diffusion with iterative denoising and first-order gradient guidance.

Background

The communication graph underlying an LLM-based multi-agent system governs its collective intelligence, but finding task-adaptive structures means searching a large combinatorial space.

Problem

Existing approaches, from heuristic pruning to autoregressive generation, lack a unified theoretical foundation for guiding how agents self-organize.

Motivation / Analysis

A principled generative view could produce better topologies than heuristics and explain why effective structures emerge.

Key Insight

Connect non-equilibrium thermodynamics with generative modeling: treat efficient collaboration as a 'cooling' process where random interactions settle into a structured low-energy equilibrium, so graph generation becomes navigating an energy landscape via score-based diffusion.

Challenges

Defining an energy landscape over graphs, generating valid task-adaptive topologies, and doing so efficiently over a large combinatorial space.

Method

MAGE is a score-based diffusion method that builds communication graphs by navigating the energy landscape through iterative denoising and first-order gradient guidance, framed as energy minimization.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on representative multi-agent benchmarks, with qualitative analysis of the generated graph structures.

Results

Reported to achieve state-of-the-art performance across representative benchmarks; generated graphs exhibit functional specialization resembling human organizations, supporting the thermodynamic hypothesis. Specific numeric metrics are not exposed in the public poster abstract.

Limitations

Public poster abstract gives qualitative SOTA claims without numbers; the thermodynamic framing's benefit over simpler generators is argued qualitatively.

Relation to the Field

A generative, theory-grounded alternative to heuristic communication-graph methods (AgentPrune) and learned graphs (GPTSwarm); part of the ICML 2026 topology-optimization cluster alongside HieraMAS.
