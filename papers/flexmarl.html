FlexMARL: Rollout-Training Co-Design for Multi-Agent RL (2026)

M215arXiv 2602.09578 (Feb 2026)pending audit source ↗
FlexMARL is an end-to-end framework that holistically co-designs rollout, training, and their orchestration for large-scale LLM-based multi-agent reinforcement learning, tackling synchronization barriers, rollout load imbalance, and underused training resources.

Background

Multi-agent RL for LLMs has advanced at the algorithm level, but the networked training infrastructure for large-scale MARL remains underexplored.

Problem

Existing RL training frameworks are built for the single-agent case and do not handle MARL-specific systems challenges: synchronization barriers, skewed rollout load, and idle training resources.

Motivation / Analysis

A disaggregated system that jointly optimizes rollout and training could remove these bottlenecks and scale LLM-based MARL to production clusters.

Key Insight

Treating rollout, training, and orchestration as one co-design problem — with an experience store and asynchronous micro-batch pipeline — eliminates synchronization barriers while preserving consistency, and agent-centric resource allocation removes imbalance.

Challenges

Removing sync barriers without breaking consistency, balancing highly skewed rollout request patterns, and allocating hardware across agents that swap states during training.

Method

A disaggregated architecture with a joint orchestrator for data flow, an experience store plus micro-batch asynchronous pipeline to eliminate sync barriers, a rollout engine with parallel sampling and hierarchical load balancing, and a training engine with agent-centric on-demand hardware binding and location-agnostic communication.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on a large-scale production cluster against existing training frameworks.

Results

Reports up to 7.3x speedup and up to 5.6x improvement in hardware utilization over existing frameworks; the batch note flags these magnitudes as not independently audited.

Limitations

No peer-reviewed venue stated and the reported speedup/utilization numbers are not independently audited; gains are measured on a specific production cluster setup.

Relation to the Field

A systems/infrastructure counterpart to algorithmic MARL co-training work like MAPoRL and Maestro, focused on the training-serving stack rather than the learning objective.
