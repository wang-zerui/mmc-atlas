# Bridging On-Device and Cloud LLMs for Collaborative Reasoning ## TL;DR - RL post-training teaches the on-device model itself when to solve locally and when to invoke a stronger cloud model. ## Problem - External routers struggle to infer reasoning difficulty from the prompt and can collapse to always-local or always-cloud behavior. ## Insight - Cloud offloading can be learned as part of the local model's post-training objective rather than bolted on as a classifier. ## Method - Uses hierarchical rewards, a group-level policy gradient, and adaptive prompt filtering to train local solve-or-offload decisions. ## Setup - Evaluates on-device-scale LLaMA and Qwen models across multiple reasoning benchmarks against external-router and full-cloud baselines. ## Results - The ICML abstract reports consistent gains over baselines and a substantially narrowed gap to full cloud inference. ## Challenges - Sparse assistance rewards and policy collapse must be controlled while preserving local solving ability. ## Limitations - The binary local/cloud setting is narrower than open model pools and introduces privacy and connectivity assumptions.

Background

Device-cloud systems pair a cheap private local model with a stronger but slower and more exposed cloud model.

Problem

External routers struggle to infer reasoning difficulty from the prompt and can collapse to always-local or always-cloud behavior.

Motivation / Analysis

The local model's own reasoning state can support a better assistance decision.

Key Insight

Cloud offloading can be learned as part of the local model's post-training objective rather than bolted on as a classifier.

Challenges

Sparse assistance rewards and policy collapse must be controlled while preserving local solving ability.

Method

Uses hierarchical rewards, a group-level policy gradient, and adaptive prompt filtering to train local solve-or-offload decisions.

Experimental Setup

Evaluates on-device-scale LLaMA and Qwen models across multiple reasoning benchmarks against external-router and full-cloud baselines.

Results

The ICML abstract reports consistent gains over baselines and a substantially narrowed gap to full cloud inference.

Limitations

The binary local/cloud setting is narrower than open model pools and introduces privacy and connectivity assumptions.

Relation to the Field

A learned self-routing cascade for edge-cloud strong-weak collaboration.
