LLMLingua (Jiang et al. 2023)

M089EMNLP 2023verified source ↗
A small aligned LM compresses prompts coarse-to-fine before the large model reads them, delegating context reduction to a weak model to cut cost.

Background

Prompts with chain-of-thought and in-context examples have grown very long, inflating inference cost and latency for large models.

Problem

Compress long prompts to a fraction of their length while preserving the large model's task performance.

Motivation / Analysis

A cheap small model can identify and remove low-information tokens, offloading context reduction from the expensive target model (strong-weak delegation).

Key Insight

Coarse-to-fine compression, driven by a budget controller and token-level iterative removal, preserves semantic integrity even at high compression ratios.

Challenges

Maintaining semantic integrity under aggressive compression; modeling interdependence between retained tokens; aligning the small compressor's distribution with the target.

Method

A small LM (instruction-tuned for distribution alignment) applies a budget controller to allocate compression across prompt components, then a token-level iterative compression algorithm removes tokens while modeling their interdependence, before the large model reads the compressed prompt.

Experimental Setup

Validated on GSM8K, BBH, ShareGPT, and Arxiv-March23.

Results

Up to 20x prompt compression with little performance loss (verified in claim_checked). The method is lossy (some information is discarded), but the reported degradation is small.

Limitations

Lossy by construction; effectiveness of the small compressor depends on alignment with the target model, and extreme compression can eventually hurt quality.

Relation to the Field

The seminal prompt-compression form of strong-weak delegation; extended by LLMLingua-2 (distilled token-classification compressor) and applied in agent-context methods like ACON and AgentDiet.
