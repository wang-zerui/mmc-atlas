Cross-Family Speculative Decoding via UAG (Fonal 2026)

M194arXiv 2026verified source ↗
An empirical study of cross-family, cross-tokenizer speculative decoding (Mistral-based Bielik-11B target with Qwen/Llama drafters) via Universal Assisted Generation token translation on Apple Silicon.

Background

Speculative decoding across model families with mismatched tokenizers, on consumer unified-memory hardware, is underexplored compared to same-tokenizer setups on high-bandwidth GPUs.

Problem

Determine whether cross-family/cross-tokenizer speculative decoding yields real speedups for a Polish LLM on Apple Silicon.

Motivation / Analysis

Practitioners may want to pair a strong target with any convenient small drafter regardless of family/tokenizer, especially on consumer hardware.

Key Insight

Context-aware token translation between tokenizers improves acceptance, but on memory-bandwidth-bound unified memory the theoretical verification savings do not amortize because both models are memory-bound.

Challenges

Translating tokens between mismatched vocabularies (naive vs context-aware); overcoming memory-bandwidth limits where sequential drafting is expensive relative to batched verification.

Method

Extends the MLX-LM framework with Universal Assisted Generation (UAG) for cross-tokenizer speculative decoding on Apple Silicon; target Bielik-11B-Instruct (Mistral-based) with drafters Bielik-1.5B (Qwen-based), Qwen2.5-1.5B, and Llama-3.2-1B, over three Polish datasets and draft lengths k in {2,4,6}, comparing naive vs context-aware token translation.

Experimental Setup

Three Polish-language datasets (Wikipedia, pl_alpaca, synthetic) across draft lengths and drafters, measuring acceptance rates and throughput.

Results

Up to 1.7x speedup on structured text but failure on varied instructions; context-aware translation consistently improves acceptance; surprisingly, the Polish-specialized 1.5B drafter underperformed general Qwen2.5/Llama drafters; verification cost did not amortize on memory-bound unified memory (verified in claim_checked). Losslessness is not established under token translation (likely lossy).

Limitations

Content-dependent gains that fail on varied instructions; unified-memory bandwidth limits verification amortization; losslessness not confirmed under cross-tokenizer translation.

Relation to the Field

An empirical, consumer-hardware counterpart to the lossless heterogeneous-vocabulary SD algorithms of Timor et al. (TLI); both target cross-tokenizer drafting but this study does not guarantee losslessness.
