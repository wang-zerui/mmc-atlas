LLMRouterBench (Li et al. 2026)

M122arXiv 2026verified source ↗
A massive unified routing benchmark (400K+ instances, 21 datasets, 33 models, 10 baselines) whose headline finding is that many recent and commercial routers fail to reliably beat a simple baseline, and the router's backbone embedding barely matters.

Background

LLM routers proliferated with many competing claims, but evaluations used inconsistent model pools and datasets, making progress hard to judge.

Problem

There is no large, unified benchmark to fairly compare routers and test whether they actually deliver over trivial baselines.

Motivation / Analysis

Sound scientific comparison requires standardized scale and strong baselines; prior benchmarks (e.g. RouterBench 2024) were smaller and gave optimistic pictures.

Key Insight

When evaluated at scale against a simple baseline and against an oracle, many sophisticated routers offer little real advantage, and embedding-backbone choice contributes little.

Challenges

Assembling 400K+ instances across 21 datasets and 33 models, and implementing 10 router baselines consistently for fair comparison.

Method

A unified benchmark harness with pre-computed model outputs over 400K+ instances, 21 datasets, and 33 models; evaluates 10 routing baselines plus commercial routers against a simple baseline and an oracle upper bound, and ablates the embedding backbone.

Experimental Setup

400K+ instances, 21 datasets, 33 models, 10 baselines, plus commercial routers; metrics on cost-quality frontier versus simple baseline and oracle.

Results

Confirms the 400K+ instances / 33 models / 10 baselines scale and the findings that routers do not reliably beat a simple baseline, that a persistent gap to the Oracle remains, and that backbone embedding choice matters little (verified in claim_checked).

Limitations

Like RouterBench, offline/static evaluation cannot capture agentic or multi-turn routing; preprint with no venue claimed.

Relation to the Field

A larger, more critical successor to RouterBench (M028); its sobering results motivate agentic benchmarks (TwinRouterBench M127) and better router designs in this family.
