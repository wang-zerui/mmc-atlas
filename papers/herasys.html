HeraSys (Li et al. 2026)

M223ICML 2026verified source ↗
HeraSys is a cross-workflow serving system for concurrent multi-tenant agentic workflows that eliminates inter-workflow redundancy via structural node merging/reuse, plus load-aware joint scheduling and resource skewing with adaptive batching.

Background

LLM serving has moved from handling single requests toward managing many concurrent, multi-tenant agentic workflows.

Problem

Existing systems focus on intra-workflow optimization and largely neglect the significant potential for inter-workflow optimization across concurrently served workflows.

Motivation / Analysis

Different concurrent workflows share structural nodes and computation, so a serving system aware of cross-workflow structure could remove redundancy and schedule globally.

Key Insight

Merging and reusing structurally identical nodes across workflows, combined with scheduling that weighs both inter- and intra-query priorities, unlocks end-to-end gains invisible to single-workflow optimizers.

Challenges

Detecting reusable structure across heterogeneous workflows, and jointly scheduling under load to reduce tail latency without inflating average latency.

Method

Structural node merging and reuse to remove cross-workflow redundancy; load-aware joint scheduling over inter/intra-query priorities; a resource skewing mechanism with adaptive batching and pipeline decomposition to cut tail latency.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on concurrent multi-tenant agentic-workflow serving under strict latency guarantees against prior serving systems.

Results

Reduces P99 latency by up to 2.17x and increases serving throughput by up to 1.85x under strict latency guarantees; accepted at ICML 2026. Verified via ICML 2026 poster abstract.

Limitations

Cross-workflow gains depend on structural overlap between concurrently served workflows; benefits are strongest under multi-tenant load with shareable nodes.

Relation to the Field

Elevates serving optimization from the single-workflow scope of Parrot/SGLang to the inter-workflow scope; complements cross-model KV-sharing work like PrefillShare and RelayCaching.
