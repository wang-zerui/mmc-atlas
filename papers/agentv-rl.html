AgentV-RL / Agentic Verifier (Zhang et al. 2026, ACL'26)

M187ACL 2026verified source ↗
Reframes reward modeling as a multi-turn, tool-augmented deliberative process with forward and backward verification agents, RL-trained so the verifier itself scales at test time under both parallel and sequential test-time scaling.

Background

Reward models and verifiers are usually single-pass scorers, whereas generators increasingly benefit from multi-turn, tool-using, test-time-scaled reasoning.

Problem

Single-pass verifiers cannot deliberate, use tools, or exploit extra test-time compute, capping their accuracy on hard problems.

Motivation / Analysis

If the verifier is itself an agent that can reason over multiple turns and scale its compute, verification quality can improve alongside generation.

Key Insight

A bidirectional design, a forward agent checking the solution and a backward agent reasoning from the answer, combined with multi-turn tool use and RL training, turns the verifier into a test-time-scalable agent.

Challenges

Designing rewards for multi-turn agentic verification and stabilizing RL training so the verifier improves under both parallel sampling and sequential deliberation.

Method

AgentV-RL casts reward modeling as a multi-turn tool-augmented deliberation with forward and backward verification agents, trained with reinforcement learning, and evaluated under parallel and sequential test-time-scaling regimes.

Experimental Setup

Comparison against state-of-the-art outcome reward models (ORMs), with test-time-scaling ablations across parallel and sequential compute.

Results

A 4B AgentV-RL variant surpasses SOTA ORMs by 25.2%, with gains under both parallel and sequential test-time scaling (verified vs abstract; ACL 2026).

Limitations

Agentic multi-turn verification costs more inference compute; reported gains center on the 4B variant and specific benchmarks.

Relation to the Field

Combines the test-time-scaling insight of Snell et al. with agentic verification (Agent-as-a-Judge, DataPRM), extending the generative-verifier line (GenRM) toward RL-trained deliberative reward models.
