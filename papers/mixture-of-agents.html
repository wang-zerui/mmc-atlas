Mixture-of-Agents (Wang et al. 2024)

M001arXiv 2024 (COLM'24)verified source ↗
Mixture-of-Agents (MoA) stacks multiple LLM agents in layers, where each agent conditions on all outputs from the previous layer, and an open-model ensemble surpasses GPT-4 Omni on AlpacaEval 2.0.

Background

Many open and proprietary LLMs are available, each with different strengths, but they are typically used in isolation. MoA asks whether the collective strengths of multiple models can be harnessed at inference time.

Problem

No single LLM dominates across queries, and combining several models naively (e.g., picking one) fails to capture their complementary capabilities.

Motivation / Analysis

The authors observe an 'collaborativeness' phenomenon: LLMs tend to produce better outputs when shown responses from other models, even weaker ones, suggesting layered aggregation can compound quality.

Key Insight

Feeding every agent the full set of prior-layer responses as auxiliary context lets later layers iteratively refine and synthesize, extracting more than any individual model.

Challenges

Selecting which models serve as proposers versus aggregators, managing latency/cost from multiple layers and calls, and avoiding degradation when weaker models inject noise.

Method

A layered architecture where each layer comprises several LLM 'proposer' agents; each agent receives all responses from the preceding layer as auxiliary information and generates a refined response, with a final aggregator synthesizing the last layer's outputs.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on AlpacaEval 2.0, MT-Bench, and FLASK, including a configuration built purely from open-source LLMs, compared against GPT-4 Omni.

Results

The open-source-only MoA reached 65.1% on AlpacaEval 2.0 versus 57.5% for GPT-4 Omni, a state-of-the-art result surpassing GPT-4 Omni (confirmed in the abstract).

Limitations

Multiple layers and agents multiply inference cost and latency; gains depend on the quality mix of proposers, and later work (Self-MoA) questions whether mixing distinct models is actually beneficial.

Relation to the Field

The foundational aggregation architecture of family B; directly challenged by Self-MoA (M036) and Co-Failure Ceiling (M135), and extended by SMoA (M120), Attention-MoA (M134), and Mixture of Complementary Agents (M118).
