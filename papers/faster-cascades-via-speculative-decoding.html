Faster Cascades via Speculative Decoding (Narasimhan et al. 2024)

M021arXiv 2024 (cs.CL)pending audit source ↗
Combines cascade deferral with speculative verification into 'speculative cascades', implementing the deferral rule via speculative execution for better cost-quality than either technique alone.

Background

Cascades and speculative decoding are two established ways to make LLM inference cheaper by interleaving a small and a large model, but they work through different mechanisms.

Problem

Cascades use a deferral rule to call the large model only on hard inputs (good cost-quality, can even beat the large model), while speculative decoding uses parallel verification (quality-neutral but no cost-quality gain); neither captures both benefits.

Motivation / Analysis

If the deferral decision of a cascade could be realized through speculative execution, one could inherit the favorable cost-quality trade-off of cascades while retaining the verification-based efficiency of speculative decoding.

Key Insight

A cascade's deferral rule can be implemented via speculative execution rather than a separate routing step, yielding 'speculative cascading' techniques; the authors characterize the optimal deferral rule and use a plug-in approximation to it.

Challenges

Characterizing the theoretically optimal deferral rule and approximating it in a way that integrates with speculative verification without sacrificing quality.

Method

Design speculative cascading techniques that fuse cascade deferral and speculative decoding; derive the optimal deferral rule and apply a plug-in approximation, using small and large models jointly.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated with Gemma and T5 model families across a range of language benchmarks, comparing against cascade and speculative-decoding baselines (including BiLD).

Results

Reports better cost-quality trade-offs than both cascading and speculative-decoding baselines; the verified claim is the speculative-deferral rule design, but the abstract gives no single headline number and specific magnitudes were not audited.

Limitations

The abstract does not report concrete speedup or accuracy figures; gains depend on the quality of the plug-in deferral approximation and the specific model pair used.

Relation to the Field

Bridges the cascade line (routing hard inputs to a larger model) and the speculative-decoding line; contrasts with pure speculative methods like BiLD and with quality-agnostic cascades.
