ProtocolBench / ProtocolRouter (Du, Zhu et al. 2025/2026)

M211ICML 2026 (accepted per arXiv v3, Jun 2026)verified source ↗
ProtocolBench isolates the effect of the agent communication protocol by holding LLM, prompts, and hardware constant while varying the protocol (A2A/ACP/ANP/Agora), and ProtocolRouter is a learnable per-module router that selects protocols from requirement and runtime signals.

Background

Multi-agent systems must choose a communication protocol, but this layer is under-studied and its impact on system behavior is rarely measured in isolation.

Problem

Protocol selection is intuition-driven and lacks standardized, quantitative guidance about how much the choice actually affects task success, latency, overhead, and robustness.

Motivation / Analysis

If the protocol layer measurably changes outcomes, teams need a benchmark that isolates its effect and, ideally, an automated way to pick the right protocol per scenario.

Key Insight

Holding everything else constant reveals that protocol choice alone has large, measurable effects, and that no single protocol is best everywhere - motivating a learnable router that adapts protocol selection per module and runtime condition.

Challenges

Fairly comparing heterogeneous protocols under identical conditions, designing scenarios that stress different failure modes, and learning a routing policy from requirement and runtime signals.

Method

ProtocolBench evaluates protocols across four dimensions (task success, end-to-end latency, message/byte overhead, robustness under failure) using scenarios like Streaming Queue and Fail-Storm Recovery; ProtocolRouter learns to pick per-scenario/per-module protocols from requirement and runtime signals.

Experimental Setup

Controlled benchmarking across multiple protocols (A2A, ACP, ANP, Agora) on the Streaming Queue and Fail-Storm Recovery scenarios plus GAIA-based tasks, with released ProtocolRouterBench and code (ulab-uiuc/AgentProtocols).

Results

Verified: in Streaming Queue, completion time varies by up to 36.5% across protocols and mean end-to-end latency differs by 3.48s; ProtocolRouter reduces Fail-Storm recovery time by up to 18.1% versus the best single-protocol baseline and raises GAIA success rates.

Limitations

Results are scenario-specific and depend on the tested protocol implementations; the router's gains are relative to the evaluated baselines and benchmark conditions.

Relation to the Field

The empirical companion to protocol surveys (Ehtesham et al.) and the semantic-view analysis (Yuan et al.), turning qualitative protocol comparison into measured performance and an automated selection mechanism.
