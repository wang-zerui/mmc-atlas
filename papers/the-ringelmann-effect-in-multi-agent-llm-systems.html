The Ringelmann Effect in Multi-Agent LLM Systems: A Scaling Law for Effective Team Size (Bertalanic & Fortuna 2026)

M156arXiv 2026 (preprint)verified source ↗
Derives a two-parameter scaling law R(N)=N_eff/N that sorts agent configs into hard-ceiling/sublinear/linear regimes, and shows negatively that 30 dense debating agents add no more diversity than one while a noise placebo matches self-correction gains; only heterogeneous teams escape the ceiling.

Background

Inference-time multi-agent scaling is often measured by nominal agent count, which conflates cost with independent evidence.

Problem

Counting agents overstates their contribution; there was no principled measure of 'effective' team size or of when adding agents actually helps.

Motivation / Analysis

A scaling law for effective agents would predict diminishing returns and identify which interventions genuinely add independent evidence.

Key Insight

Effective team size follows R(N)=N_eff/N = 1/(1+c(N-1)N^-beta), whose exponent beta defines hard-ceiling (0), sublinear (0<beta<1), or linear (beta>=1) regimes; a mean-field theorem shows peers k and rounds tau matter only via their product k·tau.

Challenges

Disentangling genuine peer-content gains from mere re-evaluation, and identifying interventions that lower the redundancy constant c.

Method

Fits the scaling law across 44 cells (model x task x condition) spanning peer debate, self-correction, a random-noise placebo, and self-consistency, over open-weight families (Qwen, Llama, Ministral, 7B-32B) plus a frontier API (Gemini).

Experimental Setup

44 cells across models, tasks, and conditions; a single N<=5 pilot used to predict the N=30 ceiling; heterogeneity vs communication-mode interventions compared.

Results

The functional form fits every condition at R^2>0.99 with only (c,beta) shifting; 30 dense debating agents produce no more answer diversity than one on MMLU-Hard; a noise placebo tracks self-correction (gains come from re-evaluation, not peer content); only heterogeneous teams lower c and escape the hard ceiling. Confirmed vs abstract.

Limitations

arXiv preprint with no venue; analysis focuses on inference-time debate/self-correction settings and open-weight models plus one frontier check.

Relation to the Field

Formalizes the diminishing returns implicit in MacNet's logistic law and echoes 'diverse beats homogeneous' (M157) and expert-underuse (M153) negative findings.
