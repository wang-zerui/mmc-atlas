AJ-Bench (Shi et al. 2026, ACL'26 Findings)

M185ACL 2026 Findingsverified source ↗
An environment-aware benchmark for Agent-as-a-Judge across search, data, and GUI domains that tests a judge's information acquisition, state verification, and process verification through tool and environment interaction.

Background

Agentic judges are being proposed to overcome static LLM-judge limits, but there is no benchmark that evaluates judging in interactive, tool-using environments.

Problem

Existing judge benchmarks present static text pairs and cannot measure whether a judge can gather information, verify environment state, or check an agent's process.

Motivation / Analysis

Judging real agent trajectories requires interacting with the environment, so evaluation must be environment-aware rather than text-only.

Key Insight

Structuring judging around three interactive competencies, information acquisition, state verification, and process verification, across diverse environments exposes where agentic judges beat static LLM judges.

Challenges

Collecting and annotating realistic agent trajectories across heterogeneous domains, and defining ground truth for interactive verification.

Method

AJ-Bench provides 155 tasks with 516 annotated trajectories across three domains (search, data, GUI) and evaluates agentic judges against LLM-as-Judge baselines on the three verification competencies.

Experimental Setup

Comparison of agentic-judge and LLM-as-Judge baselines over the 155 tasks / 516 trajectories, measuring judging accuracy per domain and competency.

Results

Environment-aware agentic judges show consistent gains over LLM-as-Judge baselines across the three domains; 155 tasks and 516 annotated trajectories (verified vs abstract; ACL 2026 Findings).

Limitations

Covers three specific domains and a moderate task count; interactive judging is more expensive than static judging.

Relation to the Field

The empirical benchmark counterpart to the Agent-as-a-Judge survey, and an interactive-environment analogue to JudgeBench; motivates environment-aware verifiers like DataPRM.
