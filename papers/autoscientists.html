AutoScientists: Self-Organizing Agent Teams for Long-Running Scientific Experimentation (Gao, Fang, Zitnik 2026)

M154arXiv 2026 (preprint)pending audit source ↗
AutoScientists is a decentralized multi-agent system (no central planner) where agents read a shared experimental state, self-organize into teams around promising hypotheses, critique proposals before spending compute, and share wins and failures.

Background

Scientific research proceeds via repeated cycles of hypothesis generation, experiment design, execution, and revision, over long horizons.

Problem

Existing AI approaches follow a single trajectory or rely on a central planner with fixed goals, limiting parallel exploration and knowledge retention.

Motivation / Analysis

Decentralized self-organization around a shared state could enable parallel hypothesis exploration while avoiding redundant compute.

Key Insight

Agents that interpret a shared experimental state can self-organize into teams around promising hypotheses and gate compute via peer critique, propagating both successes and failures to prevent duplicated work.

Challenges

Coordinating without a central planner, deciding when to commit expensive experimental compute, and retaining knowledge across long-running runs.

Method

Decentralized agents read/write a shared experimental state, form teams around hypotheses, review proposals before using compute, and broadcast outcomes (successes and failures) across the team.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on BioML-Bench (24 tasks), a GPT-training optimization task, and ProteinGym fitness prediction (217 assays).

Results

Reports 74.4% mean leaderboard percentile on BioML-Bench (+8.33% over the strongest competing agent), reaches target validation bits-per-byte 1.9x faster than Autoresearch (7 accepted improvements vs 0 for single-agent), and improves ProteinGym Spearman by +6.5% overall (+12.5% on ACE2-Spike). arXiv only, not independently audited.

Limitations

arXiv preprint with no venue; the reported magnitudes are from the abstract and not independently audited.

Relation to the Field

A decentralized, self-organizing alternative to central orchestrator-worker designs (Magentic-One, Anthropic research system), applied to autonomous science.
