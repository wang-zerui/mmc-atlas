Structured Message Passing for Multi-LLM Aggregation (Mehrabi et al. 2026)

M119arXiv 2026verified source ↗
Aggregates domain-specialized LLMs at the semantic level via a bipartite factor graph plus error-recovery-inspired message passing, using asymmetric damping to shield strong 'anchor' models from weak members, with no extra LLM calls during refinement.

Background

Smaller domain-specialized LLMs can reach expert-level performance in their niche but falter outside it, motivating ensembles that combine specialists.

Problem

Existing ensemble techniques rely on repeated prompting or cross-model refinement, incurring high computational cost and latency, and suffer 'anchor corruption' where noise from weaker models degrades the strongest expert.

Motivation / Analysis

Graph-based belief propagation from error-correcting systems offers a way to resolve disagreements at the semantic layer without repeatedly re-querying models.

Key Insight

Modeling LLMs as variable nodes and consistency criteria as check nodes in a factor graph, with asymmetric damping protecting reliable anchors, turns aggregation into a fast, scalable message-passing computation.

Challenges

Preventing majority noise from corrupting the strongest expert, and achieving aggregation without additional expensive LLM calls at refinement time.

Method

A bipartite factor graph where variable nodes are individual LLMs and check nodes evaluate consistency via diverse epistemic criteria; an error-recovery-inspired message-passing protocol iteratively resolves disagreements over output distributions, with an asymmetric damping mechanism shielding high-reliability anchor nodes; no extra LLM calls during refinement.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on MMLU, MMLU-Pro, GPQA, and MedMCQA against leading multi-agent baselines.

Results

Consistently outperforms leading multi-agent baselines while achieving 97% reduction in token usage and up to 6x fewer API calls, cutting inference time from minutes to milliseconds (confirmed vs abstract).

Limitations

Operates on output distributions and requires consistency criteria to be specified; a 2026 preprint with results on multiple-choice-style benchmarks.

Relation to the Field

An efficiency-focused, no-extra-call alternative to iterative MoA (M001) and debate; its anchor-protection theme parallels SMoA's sparsification (M120) and the anchored refinement of Self-Consistent MoA (M117).
