Orca (Mukherjee et al. 2023)

M091arXiv 2023 (Microsoft Research)verified source ↗
Distillation as strong-weak transfer: a 13B model imitates GPT-4's step-by-step explanation traces rather than just output style, a training-time coupling that later runs solo.

Background

Small models trained to imitate large foundation models often only copy surface style, misled by shallow signals, homogeneous imitation data, and weak evaluation.

Problem

Teach a small (13B) model to acquire the reasoning process of a large model like GPT-4, not just mimic its answers.

Motivation / Analysis

Rich supervision signals such as explanation traces and step-by-step thought processes convey how the teacher reasons, which should transfer more capability than output-only imitation.

Key Insight

Learning from complex explanation traces and step-by-step reasoning, with teacher assistance from ChatGPT and careful data sampling, captures reasoning rather than style and substantially closes the gap to the teacher.

Challenges

Collecting large-scale, diverse explanation traces; judicious sampling/selection of imitation data; progressive learning through easier-then-harder supervision.

Method

Orca-13B is fine-tuned on GPT-4 explanation traces, step-by-step thought processes, and complex instructions, using ChatGPT as an intermediate teacher assistant and large-scale diverse imitation data with judicious sampling (progressive learning).

Experimental Setup

Evaluated zero-shot on Big-Bench Hard (BBH), AGIEval, and professional/academic exams (SAT, LSAT, GRE, GMAT), compared to Vicuna-13B, ChatGPT, and GPT-4.

Results

Orca-13B beats Vicuna-13B by more than 100% on BBH and by 42% on AGIEval, and reaches ChatGPT parity on BBH (verified in claim_checked). As a distillation method it is inherently lossy relative to the GPT-4 teacher (still trails GPT-4).

Limitations

A lossy, training-time strong-weak transfer that still lags GPT-4; depends on quality of teacher traces; evaluation is zero-shot without chain-of-thought at inference.

Relation to the Field

Represents distillation as offline strong-weak collaboration (contrasted with inference-time draft-verify methods); the trained small model runs independently afterward, unlike LLMLingua-style runtime delegation.
