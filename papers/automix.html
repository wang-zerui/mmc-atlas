Background

Cloud LLM services offer models of many sizes; efficiently mixing them requires deciding when a small model's answer is trustworthy enough to keep.

Problem

Route context-grounded reasoning queries from a small to a large LM without training a separate difficulty predictor, using only the small model's self-assessment.

Motivation / Analysis

Prior cascades needed labeled data or dedicated scorers; a training-light approach that reuses the small model for verification is more practical, but raw self-verification is noisy.

Key Insight

Few-shot self-verification gives a usable (if noisy) correctness estimate, and treating routing as a POMDP lets a meta-verifier account for that noise when deciding to escalate.

Challenges

Self-verification is unreliable; the POMDP meta-verifier must be robust to noisy confidence to avoid over- or under-escalating.

Method

The small LM generates an answer and few-shot self-verifies it; a POMDP-based meta-verifier maps the (noisy) verification signal to a decision to accept or route the query to the larger LM.

Experimental Setup

Five context-grounded reasoning datasets using LLaMA2-13B and LLaMA2-70B (small/large pair) across five models; compared against strong cascade/routing baselines.

Results

Improves incremental benefit per cost by up to 89% over baselines across the 5 datasets, and reduces computational cost by over 50% for comparable performance (verified in claim_checked).

Limitations

Relies on the small model's self-verification ability; evaluated on context-grounded reasoning, so gains may differ on other task types.

Relation to the Field

Extends the FrugalGPT-style cascade with self-verification and a principled POMDP escalation policy; related to confidence-calibration approaches like Gatekeeper and UCCI.
