Variational Speculative Decoding / VSD (Zou et al. 2026)

M191arXiv 2026verified source ↗
Retrains the drafter via variational inference to maximize sequence-level target-model acceptance (ELBO + EM) rather than token likelihood, targeting the real multi-path decoding objective.

Background

Speculative-decoding drafters are usually trained on single greedy trajectories via token likelihood, but actual decoding verifies and ranks several sampled draft paths, creating a training-decoding mismatch.

Problem

Train the drafter so its objective matches decoding, i.e., maximize the target model's acceptance over sampled draft paths rather than token-level likelihood.

Motivation / Analysis

Tuning on single greedy trajectories ignores that decoding samples and verifies multiple paths, so likelihood is the wrong objective for maximizing accepted length.

Key Insight

Treating draft paths as latent variables and maximizing target-model acceptance yields an ELBO objective that encourages strong proposals while limiting divergence from the target distribution.

Challenges

High variance in the latent-path objective; balancing proposal strength against divergence; optimizing through sampling.

Method

VSD formulates draft training as variational inference over latent draft proposals, maximizing an ELBO for target acceptance plus a path-level utility term, optimized via EM: E-step draws Monte Carlo samples from an oracle-filtered posterior, M-step maximizes weighted likelihood using Adaptive Rejection Weighting (ARW) and Confidence-Aware Regularization (CAR).

Experimental Setup

Evaluated across LLM and multimodal LLM (MLLM) settings against EAGLE-3 and ViSpec.

Results

Up to 9.6% speedup over EAGLE-3 and 7.9% over ViSpec (verified in claim_checked). The abstract does not claim exact distribution preservation; the drafter-training objective is approximate, so it is best characterized as (potentially) lossy on the drafter side even though verification against the target still governs final acceptance.

Limitations

Requires retraining the drafter; abstract does not establish exact distribution preservation; gains over EAGLE-3 are modest (single-digit percent).

Relation to the Field

Improves the drafter-training objective underlying EAGLE-style speculative decoding, shifting from token likelihood to sequence-level acceptance; complementary to verification-side methods like PARSE.
