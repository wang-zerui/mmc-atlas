Agreement-Based Cascading (Kolawole et al. 2024, TMLR)

M034TMLRverified source ↗
Each cascade level runs a small ensemble and uses intra-level agreement as the data-dependent signal for whether to defer, beating the best single model on both cost and accuracy.

Background

Adaptive inference lowers cost by sending easy examples to small models and reserving large models for hard ones, across edge-to-cloud, cloud-serving and API-cascade settings.

Problem

Reliably deciding, per input, when a cheaper cascade level is trustworthy enough to stop, without a separately trained deferral scorer.

Motivation / Analysis

Prior cascades depend on learned confidence/deferral functions; a simpler, model-agnostic signal that also boosts accuracy would be a better drop-in replacement.

Key Insight

Agreement among an ensemble of models at a cascade level is a strong, data-dependent deferral signal, and the ensembling itself improves accuracy, offsetting its extra compute given large inter-level size gaps and parallelism.

Challenges

Justifying the overhead of running ensembles at each level; the paper argues this is offset by large model-size differences, parallel execution, and ensembling accuracy gains, and analyzes it theoretically and empirically.

Method

Agreement-Based Cascading (ABC) orders models by increasing size into a cascade; at each level an ensemble runs and their agreement decides whether to accept the answer or defer to the next larger level, acting as a drop-in replacement for a single model.

Experimental Setup

Three deployment scenarios: edge-to-cloud inference, cloud-based model serving, and API model services (compared against SOTA LLM cascades). Model/dataset specifics in full paper.

Results

Up to 14x lower edge-to-cloud communication, 3x lower cloud rental cost, and 2-25x reduction in average price per token/request versus SOTA LLM cascades (verified in claim_checked), while surpassing the best single model.

Limitations

Running ensembles adds per-level compute that only pays off under large size gaps/parallelism; specific benchmarks not in abstract.

Relation to the Field

A cascade method in the FrugalGPT/AutoMix lineage that replaces learned confidence with ensemble agreement; related to calibration-based deferral work (Gatekeeper, UCCI).
