Co-Failure Ceiling: When Does Combining Models Help? (Chen 2026)

M135arXiv 2026verified source ↗
A critique across 67 frontier models arguing gains from routing, voting, cascades, and MoA are capped by 'beta', the rate at which all models fail on the same query; average pairwise error correlation cannot identify beta, and combining rarely beats the single best model without a strong query-level routing signal.

Background

Multi-model systems (routing, voting, cascades, fusion, mixture-of-agents) are widely claimed to outperform single models.

Problem

It is unclear when combining models genuinely helps, and the common diagnostic (average pairwise error correlation, rho) may mislead about achievable gains.

Motivation / Analysis

A principled ceiling metric computable before building a router would tell practitioners the maximum achievable gain and prevent over-investment in combination.

Key Insight

Beta, the all-models-wrong rate, upper-bounds any single-answer policy's accuracy at 1 - beta; rho cannot identify beta because distributions with identical marginals and pairwise correlations can have different all-wrong rates, so single-factor models underprice the all-wrong tail.

Challenges

Estimating beta with finite samples and distinguishing genuine complementarity from format-induced co-failure.

Method

Introduces beta as a ceiling metric, provides a Clopper-Pearson finite-sample bound on beta as a certificate before training a router, and compares against a tetrachoric-calibrated single-factor model and a 67-model Gaussian copula.

Experimental Setup

67 models from 21 providers on open-ended mathematics, execution-graded code, and a GPQA-Diamond format test (multiple-choice vs free-response), with a five-judge panel (kappa 0.73-0.92).

Results

On open-ended math observed beta = 0.052 vs 0.023 predicted (~2.5x underpricing, 90% CI 1.7-3.4); code beta = 0.079; re-asking GPQA-Diamond as free-response raised beta to 0.127; at matched quality low-rho heterogeneous ensembles beat high-rho Self-MoA, and on checkable tasks combining rarely beats the single best model absent a routing signal (confirmed vs abstract).

Limitations

A 2026 preprint (cs.AI/cs.LG, no venue); conclusions center on checkable tasks and depend on the estimation and judging setup.

Relation to the Field

A theoretical ceiling that qualifies MoA (M001), voting (M017, M018, M040), and Self-MoA (M036); its low-correlation-beats-high-correlation finding supports Heter-MAD (M043) and Beyond Majority Voting's correlation-awareness (M132).
