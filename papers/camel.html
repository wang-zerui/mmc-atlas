CAMEL (Li et al. 2023)

M012NeurIPS 2023verified source ↗
CAMEL uses role-playing with 'inception prompting' to let two communicative agents autonomously cooperate on a task, also generating conversational data for studying agent behavior.

Background

Chat-based LLMs solve complex tasks but rely heavily on human guidance at each step, which is laborious and hard to scale.

Problem

There was no scalable way to get communicative agents to cooperate autonomously while staying aligned with the original human intent.

Motivation / Analysis

Reducing human intervention would enable studying the 'cognitive' processes and emergent behaviors of agent societies at scale.

Key Insight

Assigning agents complementary roles (e.g. AI user vs AI assistant) and steering them with 'inception prompting' keeps autonomous cooperation on-task and consistent with human intentions.

Challenges

Preventing role flipping, conversation drift, and deviation from the assigned task without a human in the loop.

Method

A role-playing framework instantiates a task-specifier plus role-conditioned agents guided by inception prompts; their dialogues are recorded as a scalable source of multi-agent conversational data. Open-source library released.

Experimental Setup

Studies of instruction-following cooperation across multi-agent settings; generation and analysis of conversational datasets.

Results

The abstract presents the framework, a scalable study method, and an open-source library rather than a single headline benchmark number. Verified claim: role-playing + inception-prompting framework.

Limitations

Autonomous role-play can still drift or produce degenerate loops; the contribution is primarily a framework and dataset method rather than a benchmarked performance gain.

Relation to the Field

An early foundation for role-based agent cooperation, influencing later role-specialized orchestrators (MetaGPT, ChatDev, AgentVerse).
