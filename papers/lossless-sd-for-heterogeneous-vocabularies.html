Lossless SD for Heterogeneous Vocabularies / TLI (Timor et al. 2025)

M086ICML 2025 (Oral)verified source ↗
Three new speculative decoding algorithms (including Token-Level Intersection) remove the shared-vocabulary constraint so any off-the-shelf model can draft for any target, losslessly.

Background

Standard speculative decoding requires the drafter and target to share an identical vocabulary/tokenizer, which restricts the pool of usable drafters and often forces training a drafter from scratch.

Problem

Enable lossless speculative decoding when drafter and target have different (heterogeneous) vocabularies, without training.

Motivation / Analysis

Removing the shared-vocabulary requirement lets practitioners pair any strong target with any convenient off-the-shelf small model as a drafter, greatly broadening applicability.

Key Insight

Verification can be defined over the intersection/mapping of the two vocabularies (e.g., Token-Level Intersection) so acceptance still preserves the target distribution despite mismatched tokenizers.

Challenges

Aligning distributions across different tokenizers while keeping the acceptance rule exact; heterogeneous drafters achieve lower acceptance than homogeneous ones.

Method

Three algorithms (including Token-Level Intersection, TLI) that operate across heterogeneous vocabularies, mapping/intersecting drafter and target token spaces so drafted tokens can be verified without a shared vocabulary and without any training.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on summarization, programming, and long-context tasks with off-the-shelf drafter-target pairs having different vocabularies.

Results

Up to 2.8x speedup over standard autoregressive decoding (verified in claim_checked). All three methods preserve the target distribution and are thus distribution-preserving (lossless), require no training, but yield lower acceptance than homogeneous drafters; being productionized in vLLM.

Limitations

Heterogeneous drafters give lower acceptance (and thus smaller speedups) than vocabulary-matched drafters; benefit depends on how well the two vocabularies align.

Relation to the Field

Generalizes lossless speculative decoding (Leviathan/Chen) to cross-tokenizer settings; a rigorous, lossless counterpart to the empirical cross-family UAG study (Fonal 2026).
