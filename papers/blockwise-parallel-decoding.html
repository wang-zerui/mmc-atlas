Blockwise Parallel Decoding (Stern et al. 2018)

M084NeurIPS 2018verified source ↗
The foundational draft-verify idea: predict k future steps in parallel with auxiliary heads, then back off to the longest prefix a scoring model validates.

Background

Deep autoregressive sequence-to-sequence models generate tokens strictly sequentially, creating a latency bottleneck regardless of architecture (recurrent, convolutional, or self-attention).

Problem

Reduce the number of sequential generation steps in autoregressive models that can score outputs in parallel, ideally without hurting quality.

Motivation / Analysis

Models that can process outputs in parallel (like self-attention) are wasted during sequential decoding; predicting several steps at once and validating them can exploit that parallelism.

Key Insight

Auxiliary heads can propose multiple future tokens simultaneously, and backing off to the longest prefix a scoring model agrees with recovers exact greedy output when desired.

Challenges

Training reliable multi-step prediction heads; balancing how far to predict against the quality cost of accepting more tokens.

Method

Auxiliary output heads predict tokens at multiple future positions in parallel; a scoring model validates them and the decoder commits the longest validated prefix, optionally trading exactness for more aggressive acceptance.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on self-attention (Transformer) models for machine translation and image super-resolution.

Results

Up to 2x iteration reduction over greedy with no quality loss, up to 7x with a slight performance decrease, and up to 4x wall-clock speedup for the fastest models (verified in claim_checked). The 2x setting is lossless; higher speedups are lossy.

Limitations

Requires training auxiliary prediction heads; lossless regime caps around 2x, with larger gains only at some quality cost; predates modern LLMs.

Relation to the Field

The conceptual ancestor of speculative decoding and Medusa's multiple decoding heads; established the predict-then-verify-longest-prefix template used throughout this family.
