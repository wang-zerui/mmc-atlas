LLMs Cannot Self-Correct Reasoning Yet (Huang et al. 2023, DeepMind)

M059ICLR 2024verified source ↗
A critical study showing that intrinsic self-correction (no external feedback) does not reliably improve reasoning and can even degrade it, and that multi-agent debate does not beat self-consistency at a matched sample budget.

Background

Self-Refine, Reflexion, and multi-agent debate had suggested LLMs can fix their own reasoning at test time, and such claims were being taken as established. This paper stress-tests those claims for reasoning tasks specifically.

Problem

Do LLMs actually improve their reasoning by self-correcting without any external feedback or oracle labels, and do reported debate gains hold when compute is fairly matched?

Motivation / Analysis

Many prior positive results leaked oracle information (e.g. knowing when to stop) or compared methods at unequal sample budgets, so the apparent benefit of self-correction/debate needed a controlled test.

Key Insight

When a model must decide on its own whether and how to revise, it has no reliable signal that its first answer was wrong, so intrinsic self-correction often changes correct answers to incorrect ones; and debate's gains are largely explained by consuming more samples, not by the debate mechanism itself.

Challenges

Disentangling genuine self-correction from oracle-guided stopping, and constructing a fair matched-budget comparison between debate and self-consistency.

Method

Reproduces intrinsic self-correction and multi-agent debate on reasoning benchmarks; notably reproduces Du et al.'s debate on the full GSM8K set with gpt-3.5-turbo-0301, 3 agents and 2 rounds, and compares against self-consistency using an equal number of sampled responses (matched budget).

Experimental Setup

Reasoning benchmarks including full GSM8K (and others), evaluating intrinsic self-correction, and debate vs. self-consistency under matched sampling budget.

Results

NEGATIVE: intrinsic self-correction does not improve and at times degrades reasoning accuracy; in the section 'Multi-Agent Debate Does Not Outperform Self-Consistency', debate with 3 agents/2 rounds does not beat self-consistency once the total number of samples (budget) is matched — the debate gains trace to extra samples, not to debate.

Limitations

Focuses on reasoning tasks and intrinsic (no-external-feedback) setting; self-correction with reliable external feedback or tools can still help, which the paper does not dispute.

Relation to the Field

The pivotal negative counterpoint to M002/M014/M015; its matched-budget critique is echoed and formalized by 2026 works M158 (isolated self-correction beats unguided debate) and M159 (debate cannot beat majority vote under homogeneity), and mechanistically unified by M161.
