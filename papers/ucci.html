UCCI: Calibrated Uncertainty for Cost-Optimal Cascade Routing (Kotte 2026)

M125arXiv 2026verified source ↗
A calibrated cascade router maps token-margin uncertainty to a per-query error probability via isotonic regression, then picks the escalation threshold by constrained cost minimization.

Background

Cascades escalate uncertain queries to a stronger model, but raw model confidence is miscalibrated, so escalation thresholds are chosen poorly.

Problem

How to set the escalation threshold in a cascade principledly, given that uncalibrated uncertainty leads to unnecessary or missed escalations.

Motivation / Analysis

If uncertainty were calibrated to a true error probability, the threshold could be chosen to minimize cost subject to an accuracy constraint rather than tuned heuristically.

Key Insight

Token-margin uncertainty can be calibrated into a genuine per-query error probability with isotonic regression, turning threshold selection into a constrained optimization.

Challenges

Calibrating raw margins reliably (reducing ECE) and solving the cost-minimization under an error constraint on a large workload.

Method

UCCI computes a token-margin uncertainty per query, calibrates it to an error probability via isotonic regression, then chooses the weak-to-strong escalation threshold by constrained cost minimization (minimize cost subject to a target error rate).

Experimental Setup

A 75K-query named-entity-recognition workload routed between a 4B and a 12B model; compared to a FrugalGPT-style learned threshold baseline.

Results

Cuts cost by 31% and reduces expected calibration error (ECE) from 0.12 to 0.03, beating a FrugalGPT-style learned threshold (verified in claim_checked); submitted 11 May 2026, no venue claimed.

Limitations

Demonstrated on a single NER workload with a 4B/12B pair; isotonic calibration needs held-out calibration data; preprint.

Relation to the Field

Sharpens the calibration/deferral thread of cascades (FrugalGPT, AutoMix, Gatekeeper) with a principled calibrated-uncertainty threshold.
