Position: Collaborative Agentic AI Needs Interoperability Across Ecosystems (Sharma et al. 2026)

M212ICML 2026 (position paper)verified source ↗
This ICML 2026 position paper argues that collaborative agentic AI needs minimal shared standards to avoid fragmenting into incompatible ecosystems, and proposes 'Web of Agents' as a minimal architectural foundation built from four components.

Background

Collaborative agentic AI - systems where agents autonomously perceive, plan, and act in digital environments - is expected to reshape industries, but solutions are being developed independently.

Problem

Independent, uncoordinated development is pushing the field toward a landscape of fragmented, incompatible agent ecosystems.

Motivation / Analysis

Adopting minimal shared standards now is necessary to build open, secure, web-scale, and widely adopted agentic ecosystems before fragmentation becomes entrenched.

Key Insight

Interoperability should reuse existing standards and infrastructure rather than reinvent them, and can be captured by a small set of architectural components rather than a heavyweight new stack.

Challenges

Defining a foundation minimal enough to be widely adopted yet complete enough to support real cross-ecosystem collaboration, spanning messaging, interaction, state, and discovery.

Method

Proposes the 'Web of Agents', a minimal architectural foundation with four components: agent-to-agent messaging, interaction interoperability, state management, and agent discovery, deliberately adopting existing standards where possible.

Experimental Setup

A position paper; it presents argument and architecture rather than empirical experiments or benchmarks.

Results

No quantitative results; the verified contribution is the interoperability thesis and the four-component Web of Agents architecture, framed as a first critical step toward interoperable agentic systems. Accepted to ICML 2026.

Limitations

As a position paper it argues for and sketches an architecture without implementing or empirically validating it.

Relation to the Field

Shares the anti-fragmentation motivation of the Ehtesham et al. protocol survey and complements concrete protocols like A2A and MCP by arguing for a minimal shared standard layer above them.
