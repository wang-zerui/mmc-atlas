Dynamic Model Routing and Cascading: A Survey (Moslem & Kelleher 2026)

M129arXiv 2026verified source ↗
The first dedicated 2026 survey of cross-model LLM routing and cascading (distinct from within-model MoE), taxonomizing methods by when decisions are made, what information they use, and how they are computed.

Background

Routing and cascading among distinct LLMs has grown into its own subfield, separate from mixture-of-experts routing inside a single model.

Problem

The literature is fragmented across difficulty-based, preference-based, clustering, uncertainty, RL and multimodal approaches with no unifying taxonomy.

Motivation / Analysis

A dedicated survey is needed to organize cross-model routing/cascading and clearly distinguish it from within-model MoE.

Key Insight

Methods can be organized along three axes: when the routing decision is made, what information it uses, and how it is computed.

Challenges

Covering diverse paradigms (difficulty, preference, clustering, uncertainty, RL, multimodality, cascading) under one coherent framework.

Method

A survey that taxonomizes dynamic multi-LLM routing and cascading by the when/what/how framework, reviewing difficulty-based, preference-based, clustering, uncertainty, RL, multimodal, and cascading paradigms.

Experimental Setup

Not applicable (survey); synthesizes and categorizes existing routing/cascading literature.

Results

Confirmed to be a survey of cross-model routing/cascading (not MoE) with the stated when/what/how organizing framework (verified in claim_checked); v1 23 Feb 2026 (v2 21 Apr), preprint (cs.NI/cs.CL/cs.PF).

Limitations

As a survey it provides no new empirical results; a preprint with no venue.

Relation to the Field

The organizing reference for this entire Routing & Cascades family, situating FrugalGPT, RouteLLM, bandit/RL routers, and cascade methods within a common taxonomy.
