GraphRouter (Feng et al. 2024, ICLR 2025)

M031ICLR 2025verified source ↗
GraphRouter builds an inductive heterogeneous task-query-LLM graph and predicts each response's effect and cost via edge prediction, generalizing to new LLMs without retraining.

Background

LLM selection must balance performance and cost, but existing selectors underuse the contextual relationships among tasks, queries and models.

Problem

Prior routers rely on transductive learning and cannot generalize to previously unseen LLMs or tasks without retraining.

Motivation / Analysis

Modeling routing as relational/contextual, rather than as isolated per-query classification, should capture interactions and enable inductive generalization to new models.

Key Insight

Casting tasks, queries and LLMs as nodes and their interactions as edges turns model selection into edge attribute prediction, so a new LLM is just a new node the trained model can score.

Challenges

Designing a heterogeneous graph and an edge-prediction mechanism that predicts both effect and cost and generalizes inductively to unseen LLM nodes.

Method

A heterogeneous graph with task, query and LLM nodes and interaction edges; a GNN-based edge-prediction mechanism forecasts the effect and cost of an LLM answering a query, and new LLMs are added as nodes without retraining.

Experimental Setup

Tested across three effect-cost weight scenarios and on generalization to new LLM settings and diverse tasks; baselines are existing routers. Specific model names in full paper.

Results

At least a 12.3% minimum performance improvement over existing routers across the three effect-cost weight scenarios (verified in claim_checked), plus at least a 9.5% effect boost on diverse tasks and reduced compute.

Limitations

Requires constructing and maintaining the interaction graph; benchmark/model specifics not in abstract; inductive generalization still bounded by training coverage.

Relation to the Field

Advances learned routing toward inductive generalization, contrasting with transductive routers (RouterDC, Zooter); its no-retraining-for-new-models goal is shared by Arch-Router.
