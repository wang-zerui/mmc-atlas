CARE: Confounder-Aware Aggregation (Zhao et al. 2026)

M188arXiv preprintverified source ↗
A judge-ensemble aggregation that models scores as true quality plus shared confounders (verbosity, style), separating signal from bias without ground-truth labels; naive majority/average voting can amplify correlated judge errors.

Background

Panels of LLM judges are aggregated by majority or average voting to improve reliability over a single judge.

Problem

When judges share biases (favoring verbose or stylistically ornate answers), simple aggregation amplifies rather than cancels those correlated errors.

Motivation / Analysis

Aggregation should recover true quality even when judges are systematically confounded and no ground-truth labels are available.

Key Insight

Modeling each judge's score as latent true quality plus shared confounder terms lets one identify and subtract the confounders, isolating the quality signal from correlated bias.

Challenges

Ensuring the latent quality/confounder decomposition is identifiable and accurate from finite samples without ground-truth supervision.

Method

CARE posits a latent-variable model (true quality + shared confounders) over judge scores, provides identifiability and finite-sample guarantees, and infers quality without labels; it is evaluated on continuous, binary, and pairwise judging.

Experimental Setup

Evaluation across 12 benchmarks spanning continuous, binary, and pairwise judgment formats, compared to majority/average-vote aggregation.

Results

CARE reduces aggregation error by up to 26.8% across 12 benchmarks, backed by identifiability and finite-sample guarantees (verified vs abstract).

Limitations

Depends on the confounder model being approximately correct; an arXiv preprint without a confirmed venue.

Relation to the Field

A principled successor to PoLL-style jury aggregation, addressing the correlated-bias risk that CyclicJudge and the debiasing work in Judging-the-Judges also target.
