Where Do Deep-Research Agents Go Wrong? / TELBench + DRIFT (2026)

M228arXiv 2026 (cs.AI; no formal venue listed)pending audit source ↗
Span-level error localization for long deep-research agent trajectories: TELBench is a 1,000-instance benchmark for spotting harmful error spans, and DRIFT is a claim-centric auditing framework that improves localization by up to 30 percentage points.

Background

Deep-research agents run long trajectories of search, tool use, and answer synthesis, and final-answer evaluation shows whether they succeeded but not which parts of the trajectory make the answer unreliable.

Problem

Outcome-only evaluation cannot localize where within a long trajectory an error originates, especially amid normal exploration, failed searches, tentative hypotheses, and harmless noise.

Motivation / Analysis

A process-level view that pinpoints harmful error spans is needed to diagnose and improve deep-research agent reliability beyond pass/fail metrics.

Key Insight

Tracking agent claims and verifying them against trajectory evidence isolates the spans where unsupported or conflicting claims actually corrupt the answer path, separating harmful errors from benign exploration.

Challenges

Collecting and annotating long real trajectories, converting raw logs into semantic spans, and labeling which spans are genuinely harmful versus harmless noise.

Method

The authors gather 2,790 real trajectories (two agent frameworks, three backbone models, three benchmarks), convert logs into semantic spans with LLM-assisted expert labeling to build TELBench (1,000 instances), and propose DRIFT, a claim-centric auditing framework that flags spans with unsupported or conflicting claims.

Experimental Setup

DRIFT is evaluated on TELBench for span-level error localization and first-error accuracy across model families and auditing frameworks.

Results

DRIFT improves span-level error localization and first-error accuracy by up to 30 percentage points across model families and auditing frameworks. The +30pp figure is from the abstract and is not independently audited (status verified_pending_audit).

Limitations

Scope is deep-research agents specifically; annotation relies on LLM-assisted expert review which may carry bias, and the headline gain has not been independently verified.

Relation to the Field

The span-level analogue of TraceElephant's agent/step attribution, applied to single deep-research trajectories; part of the family-Z shift from outcome metrics to process-level failure diagnosis.
