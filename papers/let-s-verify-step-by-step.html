Let's Verify Step by Step / PRM800K (Lightman et al. 2023)

M070arXiv preprint (OpenAI); ICLR 2024verified source ↗
A process reward model scoring each reasoning step is a stronger verifier for best-of-N selection than an outcome reward model.

Background

Large language models produce multi-step reasoning chains that are often unreliable, and reward models used to rank candidate solutions can be trained on the final outcome or on each intermediate step.

Problem

It was unclear whether supervising the correctness of each reasoning step (process supervision) yields a better verifier than supervising only the final answer (outcome supervision).

Motivation / Analysis

Better verifiers enable best-of-N selection and safer reasoning, and per-step feedback is more interpretable and could catch errors that outcome-only signals miss.

Key Insight

Rewarding correct reasoning at every step gives a denser, more reliable training signal than a single outcome label, producing a process reward model (PRM) that selects far better solutions.

Challenges

Process supervision requires expensive step-level human labels; the authors also show active learning is needed to make the labeling efficient.

Method

They train an outcome reward model and a process reward model on the MATH dataset, use each to re-rank sampled solutions (best-of-N), and release PRM800K, ~800K step-level human feedback labels.

Experimental Setup

Evaluation on the MATH benchmark comparing PRM vs ORM vs majority voting for solution selection, plus active-learning ablations.

Results

The process-supervised PRM solves 78% of problems from a representative MATH test subset and significantly outperforms outcome supervision; 800K step-level labels (PRM800K) are released (confirmed in abstract).

Limitations

Requires costly human step-level annotation; results are demonstrated primarily on MATH and may not transfer to all reasoning domains without new labels.

Relation to the Field

The canonical process-reward-model verifier; downstream works (Generative Verifiers, Scaling Test-Time Compute, DataPRM, AgentV-RL) build on or generalize its PRM verifier to gate generation quality.
