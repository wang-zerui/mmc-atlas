Detection Without Correction: A Two-Parameter Decomposition of Multi-Stage LLM Pipelines (Nilayam et al. 2026)

M161arXiv 2026verified source ↗
A NEGATIVE-mechanism paper that reframes a downstream agent's response as detection (should upstream content be trusted?) plus conditional generation, unifying self-correction degradation and non-replication of debate gains as one failure mode where conditional miscorrection dominates at 53-94%.

Background

Multi-stage LLM pipelines (multi-agent debate, self-correction, retrieval-augmented verification) show puzzling aggregate behaviors: accuracy plateaus, round-to-round reversals, and cross-provider divergence in debate dynamics.

Problem

Is there a single underlying mechanism that explains why self-correction degrades performance and why multi-agent debate gains fail to replicate across models and rounds?

Motivation / Analysis

Treating these phenomena as separate confounds progress; decomposing each downstream response into its constituent decisions could reveal a common cause.

Key Insight

A downstream response is two coupled decisions — detection (whether to treat upstream content as authoritative) and conditional generation (what to produce if not) — and the load-bearing failure is 'detection without correction': the model detects a problem but still cannot correct it, producing conditional miscorrections.

Challenges

Operationalizing detection vs. conditional generation as observable regimes, and demonstrating that the same threshold behavior recurs across model families, benchmarks, and both debate and self-correction.

Method

Decomposes responses into detection + conditional generation, yielding four observable response regimes; runs a nine-cell empirical grid over four model families, four benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH-500, GPQA-Diamond, AIME), and two methods (multi-agent debate and intrinsic self-correction).

Experimental Setup

Four model families x four benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH-500, GPQA-Diamond, AIME) x two methods (debate, self-correction), a nine-cell grid.

Results

NEGATIVE unification: the conditional miscorrection rate is consistently dominant at 53-94% across cohorts (the load-bearing failure), while the detection rate varies contextually by more than an order of magnitude. The detection threshold is a stable model/protocol-level regularity persisting across methods at matched benchmark difficulty, explaining self-correction degradation and non-replicating debate gains as one mechanism.

Limitations

arXiv-only; the framework diagnoses rather than fixes the failure, and the four-regime decomposition is a modeling abstraction of more complex pipeline behavior.

Relation to the Field

Provides the unifying mechanism behind the negative results of M059, M158, and M162; shares authorship/framing with the adversarial-peer study M160.
