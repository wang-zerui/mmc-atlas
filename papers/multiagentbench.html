MultiAgentBench (Zhu et al. 2025)

M116arXiv 2025 (cs.MA; no formal venue listed)pending audit source ↗
A comprehensive benchmark for LLM multi-agent collaboration and competition that uses milestone-based KPIs to measure not just task completion but the quality of coordination across multiple communication topologies.

Background

Existing agent benchmarks focus on single-agent tasks or narrow domains and do not capture multi-agent dynamics such as coordination and competition.

Problem

There was no standardized way to evaluate how well LLM agents collaborate or compete, or to compare coordination protocols on a common footing.

Motivation / Analysis

A benchmark that scores collaboration quality (not only final task success) would let researchers compare topologies and coordination strategies and diagnose where teamwork helps.

Key Insight

Measuring milestone-based KPIs during execution captures collaboration and competition quality that outcome-only metrics miss, revealing which coordination structures actually improve teamwork.

Challenges

Designing interactive scenarios plus milestone KPIs that generalize across diverse tasks, and fairly comparing coordination protocols (star, chain, tree, graph) and strategies like group discussion and cognitive planning.

Method

MultiAgentBench (built on the MARBLE framework) defines diverse interactive scenarios and milestone-based KPIs, evaluating star/chain/tree/graph topologies and strategies such as group discussion and cognitive planning.

Experimental Setup

Multiple LLMs and coordination protocols are evaluated across the benchmark's collaborative and competitive scenarios, including a research scenario.

Results

gpt-4o-mini achieved the highest average task score; the graph structure performed best among coordination protocols in the research scenario; cognitive planning improved milestone achievement rates by ~3% (a modest gain). Headline drawn from abstract/secondary sources, not fully audited.

Limitations

The collaboration gains from structure/planning are small (~3%), and status is verified_pending_audit; some numbers were not independently confirmed against the full paper.

Relation to the Field

A positive benchmarking effort measuring collaboration quality; contrasts with failure-focused benchmarks (AgentCollabBench, TraceElephant) and with the single-vs-multi-agent skepticism of Tran & Kiela.
