Should we be going MAD? (Smit et al. 2023)

M042ICML 2024verified source ↗
A negative benchmarking study finding that multi-agent debate (MAD) does not reliably beat self-consistency or multi-path ensembling and is highly sensitive to hyperparameters.

Background

Multi-agent debate has been proposed to improve LLM truthfulness and reasoning, but its advantage over simpler prompting strategies was untested at scale.

Problem

Does MAD genuinely outperform cheaper alternatives like self-consistency and ensembling, once cost, time, and accuracy are jointly considered?

Motivation / Analysis

Reliable and accurate answers remain an open problem, and MAD's claimed benefits needed rigorous comparison against strong non-debate baselines.

Key Insight

In their current form, debating systems do not reliably outperform other prompting strategies; MAD is more sensitive to hyperparameters and harder to optimize than the simpler baselines.

Challenges

Fairly tuning many debate protocols, and disentangling genuine debate benefits from hyperparameter effects such as agent-agreement levels.

Method

Benchmark a variety of debate and prompting strategies across popular datasets, analyzing cost/time/accuracy trade-offs and hyperparameter sensitivity, including tuning agent-agreement levels.

Experimental Setup

Comparison of multiple MAD systems (e.g., Multi-Persona) against self-consistency and multi-path ensembling across popular research datasets, with an open-source benchmarking repository.

Results

MAD does not reliably outperform self-consistency/ensembling; tuning agent-agreement can help and no protocol dominates all datasets (confirmed in abstract); no single headline number reported.

Limitations

Findings are for the debate protocols and datasets tested; well-tuned agent-agreement can still surpass non-debate protocols, so the verdict is 'not reliably,' not 'never.'

Relation to the Field

A skeptical counterpart within family B that elevates self-consistency (M017) as a hard baseline; its critique is deepened and partly answered by Heter-MAD (M043) via model heterogeneity.
