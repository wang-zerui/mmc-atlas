SMoA — Sparse Mixture-of-Agents (Li et al. 2024)

M120arXiv 2024verified source ↗
SMoA applies sparse mixture-of-experts principles to Mixture-of-Agents, sparsifying inter-agent information flow via Response Selection and Early Stopping plus distinct role descriptions, improving the performance-efficiency balance of dense MoA.

Background

Multi-agent LLM systems scale the number of agents, but dense all-to-all interaction between agents can hamper both efficiency and diversity.

Problem

Fully connected agent communication creates information overload and redundancy, reducing efficiency and diverging thinking as agent count grows.

Motivation / Analysis

The sparse mixture-of-experts (SMoE) paradigm improves efficiency by routing to a subset of experts; the same sparsity principle can be transferred to inter-agent communication.

Key Insight

Sparsifying which agent responses propagate, stopping early when enough signal is gathered, and assigning distinct roles preserves diversity while cutting cost, striking a better performance-efficiency balance than dense MoA.

Challenges

Deciding which responses to keep and when to stop without losing accuracy, and maintaining workload balance and diversity across agents.

Method

A Response Selection mechanism filters agent outputs, an Early Stopping mechanism halts processing to balance performance and efficiency, and distinct role descriptions (inspired by SMoE expert diversity) promote divergent thinking.

Experimental Setup

Extensive experiments on reasoning, alignment, and fairness benchmarks, with analyses of stability, scalability, and hyperparameter sensitivity.

Results

SMoA achieves performance comparable to traditional dense MoA at significantly lower computational cost, and is more stable and scalable (confirmed vs abstract; no single headline number in abstract).

Limitations

The abstract reports comparable-not-superior accuracy and gives no single headline metric; benefits hinge on tuning the selection/stopping thresholds.

Relation to the Field

A sparse-communication efficiency variant of MoA (M001) sharing the pruning/early-stopping and role-diversity ideas later echoed by Attention-MoA (M134) and the no-extra-call design of Structured Message Passing (M119).
