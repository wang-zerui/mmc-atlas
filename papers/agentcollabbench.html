AgentCollabBench (2026)

M225arXiv 2026 (cs.CL; no formal venue listed)verified source ↗
A 900-task human-validated benchmark that diagnoses process-level MAS failures under a fixed protocol, isolating four behavioral risks and revealing a synthesis bottleneck specific to converging-DAG topologies.

Background

Multi-agent pipelines can produce correct-looking final outputs even when an intermediate agent silently corrupts the reasoning chain, and outcome-based evaluation cannot detect these silent multi-hop process failures.

Problem

Existing evaluations measure only final-answer correctness and miss multi-hop process failures, so structural reliability problems remain invisible until deployment.

Motivation / Analysis

Making silent process failures measurable before deployment enables diagnosing whether reliability comes from model capability or from communication architecture.

Key Insight

MAS reliability is fundamentally structural: failures concentrate at converging-DAG nodes where an agent balancing competing parent inputs discards minority-branch constraints, so scaling model intelligence alone is no substitute for architecture.

Challenges

Building human-validated tasks that expose specific behavioral failures, and holding the collaboration protocol fixed so that observed failures are attributable to behavior and topology rather than orchestration differences.

Method

Under a fixed protocol, the benchmark of 900 tasks (software engineering, DevOps, data engineering) probes four behavioral failure modes: instruction decay, false-belief contagion, context leakage, and tracer durability.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated GPT-4.1-mini, Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite, Qwen-3.5-35B-A3B, and Llama 3.1 8B Instruct across communication topologies.

Results

No single model dominates: Qwen-3.5-35B-A3B led on tracer durability and instruction stability while GPT-4.1-mini led on leakage containment and false-belief resistance; communication topology explained 7-40% of variance in multi-hop information survival, with a synthesis bottleneck at converging-DAG nodes absent in linear chains.

Limitations

Coverage is limited to three technical task domains and four failure modes under one fixed protocol; the benchmark measures failure propensity but does not itself prescribe architectural fixes.

Relation to the Field

A failure-mode benchmark extending MAST's taxonomy into measurable behavioral tests; its structural (topology) emphasis complements TraceElephant/DRIFT attribution work and reinforces Tran & Kiela's architecture-over-capability skepticism.
