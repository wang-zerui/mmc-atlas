Mixture of Complementary Agents (Zhang et al. 2026)

M118arXiv 2026pending audit source ↗
Reframes proposer selection for the N-to-1 MoA summarizer as a combinatorial feature-selection problem, where a model's value is its complementarity with others, solved via greedy selection gauged on a small labeled set.

Background

In MoA, several 'proposer' LLMs generate responses that a 'summarizer' LLM synthesizes into an improved answer; which proposers to include is a key design choice.

Problem

Prior work selects proposers by standalone accuracy or by diversity, ignoring how proposers interact with each other and the summarizer.

Motivation / Analysis

A model's contribution should be judged by its complementarity within the ensemble rather than its individual strength, so selection should be combinatorial.

Key Insight

Proposer selection is akin to feature selection: value comes from complementarity, and complementarity can be estimated cheaply on a small labeled set.

Challenges

Standard feature-selection algorithms have prohibitive time complexity in the LLM setting, forcing computationally feasible approximations.

Method

Formulate proposer selection as combinatorial feature selection over complementarity, and investigate a broad set of computationally feasible greedy-style selection algorithms that evaluate complementarity using a small labeled set.

Experimental Setup

Experiments validating complementarity as a guiding principle for proposer selection and comparing selection algorithms on performance-cost trade-offs.

Results

Complementarity-guided selection is validated and specific greedy methods achieve the best performance-cost trade-offs in practice (confirmed vs abstract; no exact numbers vs standard MoA given in abstract).

Limitations

The abstract reports no exact numeric gains over standard MoA, requires a small labeled set for gauging complementarity, and status is verified-pending-audit; a 2026 preprint.

Relation to the Field

A proposer-selection refinement of MoA (M001) that operationalizes the diversity-vs-quality tension raised by Self-MoA (M036); complementarity echoes the low-correlation-ensemble intuition of Co-Failure Ceiling (M135).
