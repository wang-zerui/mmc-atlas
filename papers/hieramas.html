HieraMAS (Yao et al. 2026)

M216ICML 2026 (Poster)verified source ↗
HieraMAS jointly optimizes intra-node LLM mixtures ('supernodes' of heterogeneous LLMs in a propose-synthesis structure) and inter-node communication topology, using multi-level reward attribution plus graph classification for whole-topology credit assignment.

Background

LLM-based multi-agent systems benefit from good communication topology, role assignment, and LLM routing.

Problem

Existing methods optimize communication topology, role assignment, or LLM routing separately and treat each agent as a single unit, ignoring the benefit of combining multiple LLMs within one role.

Motivation / Analysis

Jointly optimizing both what happens inside a node (a mixture of models) and how nodes communicate should yield stronger and more cost-efficient systems than optimizing either alone.

Key Insight

Each functional role can be a 'supernode' of multiple heterogeneous LLMs arranged in a propose-synthesis structure, and topology selection should be treated as a holistic graph-classification problem rather than per-edge optimization to avoid reinforcing weak configurations.

Challenges

Credit assignment is hard because overall task success can wrongly reinforce weak intra-node or topology choices, so feedback must be attributed at multiple levels.

Method

A hierarchical collaboration framework that jointly optimizes intra-node LLM mixtures and inter-node topology, trained with a two-stage algorithm: multi-level reward attribution (node and system level) and graph classification for holistic topology selection.

Experimental Setup

Evaluated on reasoning and coding benchmarks against existing multi-agent optimization methods.

Results

Reported to significantly outperform existing methods and achieve better cost-performance trade-offs; the ICML poster page does not expose specific numeric metrics publicly.

Limitations

Public poster abstract gives qualitative rather than numeric results; the joint intra-/inter-node search space is large and its optimization cost is not quantified in the abstract.

Relation to the Field

Extends single-unit-per-agent graph optimizers like GPTSwarm and AgentPrune by adding intra-node model mixtures; related to OMAC and MAS-Orchestra as holistic MAS optimizers at ICML 2026.
