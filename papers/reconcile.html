ReConcile (Chen et al. 2023)

M022ACL 2024pending audit source ↗
A round-table conference of diverse LLM agents discusses over multiple rounds and uses confidence-weighted voting to reach a stronger consensus answer.

Background

LLMs still struggle with complex reasoning, and single-model or homogeneous multi-agent setups limit the diversity of perspectives. ReConcile follows the 'society of minds' idea but emphasizes model diversity.

Problem

How can collaboration among heterogeneous LLMs be structured so that diverse perspectives and confidence signals improve reasoning beyond any single agent?

Motivation / Analysis

Different model families make different errors; a round-table where agents persuade each other and weight votes by confidence should exploit that diversity to correct mistakes.

Key Insight

Combining (a) diverse LLM agents, (b) multi-round discussion where agents try to convince each other using answer-rectifying human explanation demonstrations, and (c) confidence-weighted voting yields better consensus than uniform voting.

Challenges

Coordinating heterogeneous API/open-source/domain-specific models, calibrating confidence scores for weighting, and constructing effective persuasive discussion prompts.

Method

Each round every agent is given a discussion prompt containing the grouped prior-round answers and explanations, their confidence scores, and demonstrations of answer-rectifying human explanations; agents revise their answers and a confidence-weighted vote produces the consensus. Agent combinations are flexible (API, open-source, domain-specific).

Experimental Setup

Evaluated across seven reasoning benchmarks with varied combinations of diverse LLM agents, including a MATH evaluation.

Results

Outperforms prior single-agent and multi-agent baselines by up to 11.4%, beats GPT-4 on three datasets, and gives an 8% improvement on MATH; ablations show model diversity is critical to performance. (Numbers per abstract; not independently audited.)

Limitations

Requires access to multiple diverse (often costly) models and reliable confidence estimates; per-benchmark numbers are pending independent audit in this survey.

Relation to the Field

A heterogeneous, confidence-weighted variant of multi-agent debate (M002); its emphasis on diversity + confidence prefigures the two fixes proposed by M159 (diversity-aware init + calibrated confidence).
