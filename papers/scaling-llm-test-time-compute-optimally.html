Scaling LLM Test-Time Compute Optimally (Snell et al. 2024)

M078arXiv preprint (UC Berkeley / Google DeepMind); ICLR 2025verified source ↗
Compute-optimal PRM-verifier search plus sequential revisions lets a small model beat a 14x larger one under matched FLOPs, gating quality via a verifier rather than parameters.

Background

LLM quality is usually improved by scaling parameters and pretraining compute, but test-time strategies (search against a verifier, iterative revision) offer an alternative compute axis.

Problem

It was unclear how much a model can improve by spending more compute at inference, and how to allocate that compute optimally per problem.

Motivation / Analysis

If additional test-time compute can substitute for a much larger model, deployment becomes cheaper and adaptive to problem difficulty.

Key Insight

Choosing the best test-time strategy depends on prompt difficulty; a compute-optimal policy that adapts between PRM-verifier search and sequential revision can dramatically improve efficiency, letting small models rival much larger ones.

Challenges

Estimating per-prompt difficulty to pick the right strategy; balancing parallel sampling against sequential revision under a fixed compute budget.

Method

The authors analyze two test-time compute mechanisms, PRM-verifier search over samples and a model that iteratively revises its answer, and derive a compute-optimal scaling strategy that adapts allocation to problem difficulty, comparing against best-of-N and larger models under FLOPs-matching.

Experimental Setup

Experiments on MATH-style reasoning measuring accuracy versus compute for the adaptive policy, best-of-N baselines, and a FLOPs-matched larger model.

Results

The compute-optimal strategy is over 4x more efficient than a best-of-N baseline, and under FLOPs-matched comparison a small model outperforms a model 14x larger on easier problems (confirmed in abstract).

Limitations

Gains concentrate on easier/medium problems; hardest problems still benefit from more parameters, and difficulty estimation adds overhead.

Relation to the Field

Shows verifier-gated test-time search as a lever complementary to parameter scaling; underpins the test-time-scaling verifiers DataPRM and AgentV-RL and relies on PRM verifiers from Let's-Verify.
