RouterDC (Chen et al. 2024, NeurIPS 2024)

M030NeurIPS 2024verified source ↗
A query-based router trained with dual contrastive losses (sample-LLM and sample-sample) assembles frozen off-the-shelf LLMs, beating the best single model and prior routers.

Background

Routing among complementary off-the-shelf LLMs can outperform any individual model when the router picks the right one per query.

Problem

Existing routers struggle when multiple LLMs perform well on the same query, producing ambiguous or unstable routing decisions.

Motivation / Analysis

A better training objective is needed to make the router's query representation discriminative both across models and across similar queries.

Key Insight

Dual contrastive learning, one loss aligning queries with well-performing LLMs and another pulling together similar queries, yields robust routing when several models are viable.

Challenges

Handling ties where multiple LLMs are correct, and training stable query/LLM embeddings that generalize out of distribution.

Method

RouterDC pairs an encoder with learnable LLM embeddings and trains with two contrastive losses: a sample-LLM loss (query vs candidate models) and a sample-sample loss (query vs query); at inference the encoder scores the query against LLM embeddings to select one.

Experimental Setup

Assembles multiple frozen off-the-shelf LLMs; evaluated on in-distribution and out-of-distribution task suites against top individual LLMs and prior routing methods.

Results

Outperforms the best individual LLM and prior routers by +2.76% in-distribution and +1.90% out-of-distribution (verified in claim_checked).

Limitations

Requires training data per model pool; specific model/benchmark names are in the paper. Gains, while consistent, are modest in absolute terms.

Relation to the Field

A learned query-based router in the lineage of Zooter and RouteLLM, distinguished by its dual-contrastive objective; benchmarked in later routing surveys.
