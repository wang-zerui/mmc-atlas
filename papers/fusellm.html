FuseLLM / Knowledge Fusion of LLMs (Wan et al. 2024)

M039ICLR 2024verified source ↗
FuseLLM combines the capabilities of multiple pre-trained source LLMs into a single target model by distilling their generative probability distributions via lightweight continual training, even across differing architectures.

Background

Training LLMs from scratch is costly and redundant, and directly merging model weights is impractical when architectures differ.

Problem

How to transfer and combine the distinct strengths of several existing LLMs into one model without weight merging or full retraining.

Motivation / Analysis

The generative probability distributions of source LLMs externalize their collective knowledge, which can be distilled into a target that potentially exceeds every source model.

Key Insight

Knowledge fusion via probabilistic distributions, coupled with token alignment across vocabularies, lets a single target inherit complementary strengths rather than merely imitating one teacher.

Challenges

Aligning tokens across heterogeneous vocabularies/architectures and ensuring the fused target surpasses (not just matches) the best source model.

Method

Leverage the generative distributions of multiple source LLMs (e.g., Llama-2, MPT, OpenLLaMA), align their tokens, and distill the combined knowledge into a target model through lightweight continual training.

Experimental Setup

Validated by fusing three architecturally distinct LLMs (Llama-2, MPT, OpenLLaMA) across various benchmarks and tasks.

Results

Fusion improves the target across reasoning, commonsense, and code generation, with the target expected to exceed source models (unlike standard single-teacher distillation); confirmed in abstract, no single headline number given.

Limitations

Requires continual training (not fully training-free) and access to source-model distributions; the abstract reports qualitative gains rather than a headline metric.

Relation to the Field

An 'ensemble-before-inference' knowledge-fusion approach (per survey M041) distinct from inference-time fusers DeePEn (M037) and PackLLM (M038); collapses many models into one rather than aggregating at query time like MoA (M001).
