Beyond Majority Voting (Ai et al. 2026)

M132ICML'26verified source ↗
A training-free aggregation framework that beats equal-weight majority voting by using both first- and second-order information via two algorithms, Optimal Weight and Inverse Surprising Popularity, with theoretical guarantees.

Background

Multi-agent reasoning aggregates answers across multiple LLMs, and standard practice is equal-weight majority voting.

Problem

Majority voting treats all answers equally, ignoring latent heterogeneity in model reliability and correlation across models.

Motivation / Analysis

Accounting for how reliable and how correlated models are (second-order information) should yield a better aggregate than naive voting.

Key Insight

Leveraging both first-order (per-model reliability) and second-order (cross-model correlation) information provably mitigates majority voting's limitations under mild assumptions, without training.

Challenges

Estimating reliability and correlation without labels/training, and doing so with provable guarantees.

Method

Two training-free algorithms, Optimal Weight (OW) and Inverse Surprising Popularity (ISP), that leverage first- and second-order information to weight and aggregate model answers, backed by theoretical analysis.

Experimental Setup

Validated on synthetic datasets, LLM fine-tuning benchmarks UltraFeedback and MMLU, and a real-world healthcare setting (ARMMAN).

Results

OW and ISP consistently outperform standard majority-voting baselines with provable guarantees (confirmed; arXiv v1 Oct 2025, v2 May 2026, accepted to ICML 2026); the abstract reports qualitative consistent improvement rather than a single headline number.

Limitations

Guarantees rest on mild stated assumptions, and the abstract gives no single headline accuracy figure; requires estimating higher-order statistics.

Relation to the Field

A principled successor to plain majority voting (self-consistency M017, More Agents M018) whose correlation-awareness aligns with the Co-Failure Ceiling (M135) and the trace-correlation argument of Self-Consistent MoA (M117).
