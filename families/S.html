S. Surveys & Related Work

22 papers
Surveys, taxonomies, and systematic studies of the field.

This family collects the surveys, taxonomies, and systematic studies of the field — the meta-literature that maps multi-model and multi-agent LLM collaboration. Three pillars are densely covered: (1) LLM-based multi-agent systems in general — progress/challenges (M301, IJCAI'24; M303), application-oriented advances (M302), and the collaboration-mechanism taxonomy (M300); (2) model combination — merge/ensemble/cooperate (M304), ensemble learning for text & code (M310), routing & cascading (M129); and (3) evaluation — LLM-as-a-judge (M306, M307), agent evaluation & benchmarking (M308, M313, KDD'25), and Agent-as-a-Judge (M184). Communication protocols (M305; and M096) and agent optimization (M312) are newly but actively surveyed as of 2025, and the AI-agents-vs-agentic-AI distinction (M311, Information Fusion'25) frames the terminology.

Thin spots the surveys themselves flag. Multi-agent debate has only a short review (M309) rather than a comprehensive treatment; MARL specifically for LLMs is covered indirectly through broader optimization/orchestration surveys (M165/M165) rather than a canonical one; and cross-cutting concerns — the cost/economics of multi-model collaboration, safety/security, and standardized benchmarks for collaboration quality — are repeatedly named as open gaps rather than surveyed in depth. The field is maturing fast on taxonomy and evaluation but still lacks consolidated, comparative empirical grounding — which is exactly the gap this atlas's per-paper, matched-compute reading tries to fill.

2026 surveys. The meta-literature now includes LLM-MAS collaboration/failure/self-evolution (M314), model merging and fusion (M315), multi-agent RL (M165), memory/context (M317), agent harness design (M318), security (M319), communication protocols (M320), and workflow optimization (M321). Broad serving/KV surveys are useful adjacent maps but are excluded from this collaboration corpus unless their unit of analysis is the collaborative workflow itself.
