A. Routing & Cascades

50 papers
Route or escalate each query/turn among models.

Base. FrugalGPT (M003), RouteLLM (M004), and RouterBench (M028/M113) established query-level cost-quality routing and its evaluation. 2026 expansion. The field now has four distinct fronts. (1) *Open and changing model pools*: UniRoute (M343) adds unseen models without retraining; ProxRouter (M344), InferenceDynamics (M351), TRouter (M352), DecoR (M353), SemiRouter (M358), and ContextualRouter (M359) tackle outliers, profiles, cold start, sparse supervision, and historical retrieval. (2) *Stateful agentic routing*: DiSRouter (M341) distributes the decision into models; MTRouter (M350), EvoRoute (M354), Online Multi-LLM Selection (M361), Agentic Routing (M364), and Iterative Critique Routing (M367) choose repeatedly from execution history; AgentRouter (M349), LatentGate (M356), and tool-provider routing (M369) expand the action from model to agent or tool. (3) *Formal resource and risk control*: R2-Router (M345) jointly chooses model and reasoning length; RACER (M346) and Conformal Routing (M362) add distribution-free set/violation guarantees; FLARE (M355) and robust batch routing (M370) bind routing to latency and serving capacity. AlphaRouter (M347) pushes the decision down to token level. (4) *Evaluation and security*: LLMRouterBench (M122) shows many routers fail to beat simple baselines; Route to Rome (M357) demonstrates denial-of-wallet attacks, while Router-LLM Fragility (M360) exposes category shortcuts and unsafe routing. The durable shift is from a query classifier to a stateful policy over model, agent, tool, subset, compute, and stopping. The bottleneck remains trustworthy signals, labels, and verification—not router sophistication alone.

Papers in this family

SeqRoute (Xu et al. 2026)
SeqRoute makes budget-aware LLM routing sequential, framing a multi-turn session as a finite-horizon MDP solved with offline RL so the router learns 'delayed gratification' and avoids spending its whole budget early.
M121arXiv 2026
LLMRouterBench (Li et al. 2026)
A massive unified routing benchmark (400K+ instances, 21 datasets, 33 models, 10 baselines) whose headline finding is that many recent and commercial routers fail to reliably beat a simple baseline, and the router's backbone embedding barely matters.
M122arXiv 2026
LLM Router: Rethinking Routing with Prefill Activations (Varshney et al. 2026)

M123arXiv 2026
Switchcraft (Agarwal et al. 2026)

M124arXiv 2026
UCCI: Calibrated Uncertainty for Cost-Optimal Cascade Routing (Kotte 2026)

M125arXiv 2026
