B. Ensembling & Aggregation

20 papers
Fuse, rank, or vote many outputs into one.

Base. Self-consistency (M017) and More Agents (M018) vote; LLM-Blender (M009)
ranks-then-fuses; Mixture-of-Agents (M001) layers proposers under an aggregator
(open-model MoA 65.1% vs GPT-4o 57.5%). Logit/distribution fusion (DeePEn M037,
PackLLM M038, FuseLLM M039) works across vocabularies. 2026 SOTA. Aggregation
got theory and sharper diversity accounting: Beyond Majority Voting (M132, ICML'26)
uses first+second-order info (Optimal Weight / Inverse Surprising Popularity) to
beat equal-weight voting with guarantees; RL-Focal (M218, ICML'26) learns a
per-query error-diverse ensemble + a conflict-resolving fusion agent (+8.48% over
best single); Self-Consistent MoA (M117) argues the aggregation unit should be the
reasoning *trace*, and that single-model perturbation traces beat heterogeneous
pools (an "aggregation paradox"); Structured Message Passing (M119) uses a factor
graph with asymmetric damping to shield strong anchors (97% token cut). Contested
lever: Self-MoA (M036) already showed mixing can *hurt* (best-model self-mix +6.6%),
and Are-More-Calls (M040) proved voting is non-monotone in call count — themes the
2026 co-failure work (§4) generalizes.

Papers in this family

Self-Consistent Mixture of Agents / Trace-Level Synthesis (Fadnavis et al. 2026)
Argues the unit of aggregation should be the reasoning TRACE, not the final answer: an aggregator reading full traces recovers correct solutions even under unanimous agreement (the 'aggregation paradox'), and single-model perturbation traces beat heterogeneous model pools.
M117arXiv 2026
Mixture of Complementary Agents (Zhang et al. 2026)
Reframes proposer selection for the N-to-1 MoA summarizer as a combinatorial feature-selection problem, where a model's value is its complementarity with others, solved via greedy selection gauged on a small labeled set.
M118arXiv 2026
Structured Message Passing for Multi-LLM Aggregation (Mehrabi et al. 2026)
Aggregates domain-specialized LLMs at the semantic level via a bipartite factor graph plus error-recovery-inspired message passing, using asymmetric damping to shield strong 'anchor' models from weak members, with no extra LLM calls during refinement.
M119arXiv 2026
Attention-MoA (Wen et al. 2026)
A Mixture-of-Agents variant adding Inter-Agent Semantic Attention plus inter-layer residual synthesis and adaptive early stopping to curb information loss in deep MoA layers, reaching 91.15% LC win-rate on AlpacaEval 2.0 and 8.83 on MT-Bench with small open models.
M134arXiv 2026
Co-Failure Ceiling: When Does Combining Models Help? (Chen 2026)
A critique across 67 frontier models arguing gains from routing, voting, cascades, and MoA are capped by 'beta', the rate at which all models fail on the same query; average pairwise error correlation cannot identify beta, and combining rarely beats the single best model without a strong query-level routing signal.
M135arXiv 2026
Self-MoA / Rethinking Mixture-of-Agents (Li et al. 2025)
A negative result for mixing: aggregating repeated samples from the single best-performing model (Self-MoA) beats standard Mixture-of-Agents that mixes different LLMs.
M036arXiv 2025
LLM Ensemble Survey (Chen et al. 2025)
The first systematic survey of LLM ensembling, organizing methods by timing into ensemble-before-, during-, and after-inference categories with a curated resource repository.
M041arXiv 2025 (ACL'25 findings)
Stop Overvaluing Multi-Agent Debate / Heter-MAD (Wang et al. 2025)
A position/evaluation paper arguing multi-agent debate is overvalued: across 5 MAD methods x 9 benchmarks x 4 models it often fails to beat CoT/self-consistency at far higher compute, but model heterogeneity (Heter-MAD) recovers gains.
M043arXiv 2025
Beyond Majority Voting (Ai et al. 2026)
A training-free aggregation framework that beats equal-weight majority voting by using both first- and second-order information via two algorithms, Optimal Weight and Inverse Surprising Popularity, with theoretical guarantees.
M132ICML'26
LLMBoost (Chen et al. 2025)
A boosting-style ensemble fine-tuning framework that fuses predecessor models' hidden states via cross-model attention under an error-suppression chain-training objective, with near-parallel layer-pipelined inference, going beyond black-box output voting.
M133arXiv 2025
Mixture-of-Agents (Wang et al. 2024)
Mixture-of-Agents (MoA) stacks multiple LLM agents in layers, where each agent conditions on all outputs from the previous layer, and an open-model ensemble surpasses GPT-4 Omni on AlpacaEval 2.0.
M001arXiv 2024 (COLM'24)
More Agents Is All You Need / Agent Forest (Li et al. 2024)
A simple sampling-and-voting method ('Agent Forest') shows LLM performance scales with the number of instantiated agents, and this gain is orthogonal to other enhancement techniques.
M018TMLR 2024
DeePEn (Huang et al. 2024)
DeePEn is a training-free ensemble that fuses heterogeneous LLMs' per-step probability distributions by mapping them into a shared 'relative representation' space that resolves vocabulary mismatch.
M037NeurIPS 2024
PackLLM (Mavromatis et al. 2024)
PackLLM ('Pack of LLMs') fuses arbitrary user-specified LLMs at test time by weighting each model according to how well it minimizes perplexity on the input prompt, with no trained fusion module.
M038arXiv 2024
FuseLLM / Knowledge Fusion of LLMs (Wan et al. 2024)
FuseLLM combines the capabilities of multiple pre-trained source LLMs into a single target model by distilling their generative probability distributions via lightweight continual training, even across differing architectures.
M039ICLR 2024
Are More LLM Calls All You Need? (Chen et al. 2024)
A scaling-laws study showing that majority-vote aggregation (Vote and Filter-Vote) is non-monotone in the number of LM calls: accuracy first rises then falls as tasks mix easy and hard queries.
M040NeurIPS 2024
Should we be going MAD? (Smit et al. 2023)
A negative benchmarking study finding that multi-agent debate (MAD) does not reliably beat self-consistency or multi-path ensembling and is highly sensitive to hyperparameters.
M042ICML 2024
SMoA — Sparse Mixture-of-Agents (Li et al. 2024)
SMoA applies sparse mixture-of-experts principles to Mixture-of-Agents, sparsifying inter-agent information flow via Response Selection and Early Stopping plus distinct role descriptions, improving the performance-efficiency balance of dense MoA.
M120arXiv 2024
LLM-Blender (Jiang et al. 2023)
LLM-Blender ensembles open-source LLMs by first ranking candidate outputs pairwise (PairRanker) and then generatively fusing the top-k into an improved answer (GenFuser).
M009ACL 2023
Self-Consistency (Wang et al. 2022)
Self-consistency samples multiple diverse chain-of-thought reasoning paths from a single model and returns the majority-voted answer, a homogeneous single-model ensemble.
M017ICLR 2023
