F. Strong-Weak Efficiency

6 papers
Weak model does bulk work; strong model verifies or refines.

Base. The one family with formal guarantees. Speculative decoding
(M005/M006) is exact-distribution 2–3×; EAGLE (M007) 2.7–6.5×; a large lossless
family (SpecInfer M081, Staged M082, Self-Speculative M083, Lookahead M080, CS
Drafting M087, REST M085, heterogeneous-vocab TLI M086); lossy siblings are
flagged as such (Medusa-2 M079, BiLD M088). Prompt/context compression (LLMLingua
M089/M090) and distillation (Orca M091) are strong→weak transfers, and agentic
context pruning (AgentDiet M092, ACON M093) extends this to trajectories.
2026 SOTA. The 2026 crop sharpens the lossless/lossy line the survey insists
on: lossless — Saguaro (M190, ICLR'26) breaks the draft/verify dependency by
pre-speculating verification (~30% over spec-decode, up to 5×); BASTION (M192)
budget-aware tree block-diffusion drafting, training-free, up to 6.61×.
Lossy / approximate — VSD (M191) retrains the drafter to maximize acceptance
(no distribution guarantee); PARSE (M193) semantic prefix verification
("negligible" but nonzero degradation); TreeFlash (M195) approximates the AR
distribution; cross-family UAG (M194) establishes no losslessness under token
translation; ConfLayers (M196) is framed as a speed–quality tradeoff. The takeaway
is unchanged and reinforced: coupling delivers robust gains because the
verification step carries a structural (distribution-preserving) guarantee.

An inference-time trajectory-reduction method that automatically prunes redundant, useless, and expired information from a software agent's growing context to cut input-token cost without hurting performance.
