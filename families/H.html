Learn the router, graph, or coordination policy.

Base. Learn the graph/policy instead of hand-designing it: GPTSwarm (M019),
ADAS (M100), AFlow (M101), TextGrad (M102), DSPy (M103); MARL co-training
(MAPoRL M104), comm-efficiency training (Optima M105, AgentPrune M107), and online
drafter retraining (M108). 2026 SOTA. A wave of ICML'26 optimizers: HieraMAS
(M216) jointly optimizes intra-node LLM *mixtures* + inter-node topology;
MAS-Orchestra + MASBENCH (M217) casts MAS design as function-calling RL and — the
key negative — finds MAS gains are not universal (depend on task structure,
verification, orchestrator/subagent capability); MAGE (M219) generates comm graphs
via score-based diffusion; OMAC (M220, Oral) jointly optimizes 5 dimensions;
RL-Focal (M218) for ensembles. Two 2026 surveys (M214/M165) organize RL-for-MAS
and flag that no RL method trains the "when to stop" decision, and FlexMARL
(M215) tackles the systems side of MAS-RL.

Papers in this family

FlexMARL: Rollout-Training Co-Design for Multi-Agent RL (2026)
FlexMARL is an end-to-end framework that holistically co-designs rollout, training, and their orchestration for large-scale LLM-based multi-agent reinforcement learning, tackling synchronization barriers, rollout load imbalance, and underused training resources.
M215arXiv 2602.09578 (Feb 2026)
HieraMAS (Yao et al. 2026)
HieraMAS jointly optimizes intra-node LLM mixtures ('supernodes' of heterogeneous LLMs in a propose-synthesis structure) and inter-node communication topology, using multi-level reward attribution plus graph classification for whole-topology credit assignment.
M216ICML 2026 (Poster)
MAS-Orchestra + MASBENCH (Ke et al. 2026)
MAS-Orchestra casts multi-agent-system design as function-calling reinforcement learning that generates an entire MAS at once (subagents abstracted as callable functions), and introduces MASBENCH, a controlled benchmark over five axes (Depth, Horizon, Breadth, Parallel, Robustness).
M217ICML 2026 (Poster)
RL-Focal: Dynamic Optimization of LLM Ensembles (Tekin et al. 2026)
RL-Focal is a two-stage reinforcement learning framework where a Decider agent picks a small, error-diverse ensemble per query and a Fusion agent learns to resolve cross-LLM reasoning conflicts, guided by a focal-diversity metric.
M218ICML 2026 (Poster)
MAGE: Multi-Agent Comm Graph Generation via Score-Based Diffusion (Zhao et al. 2026)
MAGE reframes multi-agent communication-graph design as an energy-minimization problem grounded in non-equilibrium thermodynamics, generating task-adaptive topologies via score-based diffusion with iterative denoising and first-order gradient guidance.
M219ICML 2026 (Poster)
OMAC: Holistic Optimization Framework for LLM-Based MAS (Li et al. 2026)
OMAC is a general framework that holistically optimizes five key dimensions of agent functionality and collaboration structure, using two actors (a Semantic Initializer and a Contrastive Comparator) to optimize any single dimension plus an algorithm for joint multi-dimension optimization.
M220ICML 2026 (Oral)
ADAS / Meta Agent Search (Hu et al. 2024)
ADAS (Automated Design of Agentic Systems) has a meta agent write agents as code, evaluate them, and store them in a growing archive, iteratively inventing novel building blocks and workflows in a Turing-complete search space.
M100ICLR 2025
AFlow (Zhang et al. 2024)
AFlow reframes agentic-workflow design as a search over code-represented workflows and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to automatically discover high-performing workflows without manual setup.
M101ICLR 2025 (Oral)
TextGrad (Yuksekgonul et al. 2024/2025)
TextGrad performs automatic 'differentiation' via text, backpropagating natural-language feedback ('textual gradients') from LLMs through a compound-system computation graph to optimize prompts, code, and other variables with a PyTorch-like API.
M102Nature (2025); arXiv 2406.07496
MAPoRL (Park et al. 2025)
MAPoRL is a post-training paradigm that co-trains multiple LLMs with multi-agent reinforcement learning: agents independently answer, then debate, and a verifier reward that rewards correct answers and corrective/persuasive discussion is maximized via MARL.
M104ACL 2025
Maestro / CLPO (Yang et al. 2025)
Maestro structurally decouples exploration (parallel Execution Agents) from synthesis (a Central Agent) and trains them with Conditional Listwise Policy Optimization (CLPO), which separates the strategic 'which output' decision from listwise-ranked rationales for cleaner credit assignment.
M106arXiv 2511.06134
GPTSwarm (Zhuge et al. 2024)
GPTSwarm ('Language Agents as Optimizable Graphs') models LLM agents and multi-agent societies as computational graphs and introduces automatic optimizers that jointly refine node prompts and edge connectivity.
M019ICML 2024 (oral)
DSPy (Khattab et al. 2023)
DSPy is a programming model that abstracts LM pipelines as text transformation graphs of declarative, parameterized modules, plus a compiler that bootstraps demonstrations/finetuning to optimize any pipeline against a metric instead of hand-tuned prompts.
M103ICLR 2024
Optima (Chen et al. 2024)
Optima improves both effectiveness and communication efficiency of LLM multi-agent systems via an iterative generate-rank-select-train loop (SFT/DPO plus MCTS-inspired data generation) with a reward balancing task success, token cost, and readability.
M105arXiv 2410.08115 (under review)
AgentPrune / 'Cut the Crap' (Zhang et al. 2024)
AgentPrune identifies and formally defines communication redundancy in LLM multi-agent systems and performs one-shot pruning on the spatial-temporal message-passing graph to remove redundant or malicious messages, yielding a token-economic, high-performing topology.
M107arXiv 2410.02506
Online Speculative Decoding (Liu et al. 2023)
Online Speculative Decoding continuously retrains the draft model(s) on the live user-query distribution using knowledge distillation and spare serving-cluster FLOPs, closing the train/serve distribution shift and enabling per-topic specialist drafters.
