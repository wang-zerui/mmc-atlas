C. Role-Specialized Orchestration

18 papers
Planner/worker/subagent teams via a manager or pipeline.

Base. Named-role teams: MetaGPT (M011), ChatDev (M050), AutoGen (M010),
CAMEL (M012), AgentVerse (M051); MacNet (M052) scaled to 1000+ agents with a
logistic "collaborative scaling law"; Anthropic's orchestrator-worker system
(M013) reported +90.2% over single Opus 4 at ~15× token cost. 2026 SOTA.
Two opposite currents. Constructive: dynamic, self-organizing orchestration —
AOrchestra (M151, ICML'26) spawns sub-agents on the fly from a 4-tuple (+16.28%);
AdaptOrch (M152) argues topology dominates model choice under LLM convergence;
HERA (M155) and AutoScientists (M154) self-organize teams without a central
planner. Destructive: the 2026 negative results land hardest here — Multi-Agent
Teams Hold Experts Back (M153, ICML'26) shows teams fail to match their best
expert (up to −41.1%) even when told who the expert is; the Ringelmann-effect
scaling law (M156) finds "30 dense debating agents produce no more diversity than
one"; and the diversity account (M157) shows 2 diverse agents ≈ 16 homogeneous.
Orchestration helps when structure is dynamic and heterogeneous, and backfires
when it forces homogeneous consensus.

Papers in this family

AOrchestra: Automating Sub-Agent Creation for Agentic Orchestration (Ruan et al. 2026)
AOrchestra has a central orchestrator that spawns task-specific sub-agents on the fly from a unified four-tuple (Instruction, Context, Tools, Model), replacing manually predefined static roles.
M151ICML 2026
AdaptOrch: Task-Adaptive Multi-Agent Orchestration in the Era of LLM Performance Convergence (Yu 2026)
AdaptOrch argues that as provider LLMs converge on benchmarks the orchestration topology, not model choice, dominates, and dynamically routes each task DAG to a parallel/sequential/hierarchical/hybrid topology.
M152arXiv 2026 (preprint)
Multi-Agent Teams Hold Experts Back (Pappu et al. 2026)
A negative result: self-organizing LLM teams consistently fail to match their best individual expert (up to 41.1% loss on ML benchmarks) even when told which agent is the expert; the bottleneck is expert leveraging, not identification.
M153ICML 2026
AutoScientists: Self-Organizing Agent Teams for Long-Running Scientific Experimentation (Gao, Fang, Zitnik 2026)
AutoScientists is a decentralized multi-agent system (no central planner) where agents read a shared experimental state, self-organize into teams around promising hypotheses, critique proposals before spending compute, and share wins and failures.
M154arXiv 2026 (preprint)
HERA / Experience as a Compass: Multi-agent RAG with Evolving Orchestration (Li & Ramakrishnan 2026)
HERA is a hierarchical multi-agent RAG framework that jointly evolves query-specific agent topologies (reward-guided sampling) and role-specific prompts (Role-Aware Prompt Evolution) without gradient training, reporting emergent self-organization into compact high-utility networks.
M155arXiv 2026 (preprint)
The Ringelmann Effect in Multi-Agent LLM Systems: A Scaling Law for Effective Team Size (Bertalanic & Fortuna 2026)
Derives a two-parameter scaling law R(N)=N_eff/N that sorts agent configs into hard-ceiling/sublinear/linear regimes, and shows negatively that 30 dense debating agents add no more diversity than one while a noise placebo matches self-correction gains; only heterogeneous teams escape the ceiling.
M156arXiv 2026 (preprint)
Understanding Agent Scaling in LLM-Based Multi-Agent Systems via Diversity (2026)
An information-theoretic account of why adding agents saturates: homogeneous outputs are correlated so gains cap at task uncertainty, while heterogeneous agents add complementary channels; empirically 2 diverse agents match or exceed 16 homogeneous ones.
M157arXiv 2026 (preprint)
Anthropic Multi-Agent Research System (2025)
An orchestrator-worker research system where a Claude Opus 4 lead agent spawns parallel Claude Sonnet 4 subagents that each search independently and return filtered findings for synthesis.
M013Anthropic engineering blog (2025)
MacNet / Scaling LLM Multi-Agent Collaboration (Qian et al. 2024)
MacNet organizes LLM agents as directed acyclic graphs, scales to 1000+ collaborating agents, and identifies a 'collaborative scaling law' of logistic quality growth, with small-world/irregular topologies performing best.
M052ICLR 2025
MetaGPT (Hong et al. 2023)
MetaGPT encodes human Standardized Operating Procedures (SOPs) into prompt sequences and an assembly-line role assignment to reduce cascading hallucination in LLM agent teams.
M011ICLR 2024 (oral)
ChatDev (Qian et al. 2023/ACL 2024)
ChatDev is a chat-powered software-development framework where role-specialized LLM agents (CEO/CTO/programmer/tester) collaborate along a 'chat chain' with communicative dehallucination to build software end-to-end.
M050ACL 2024
AgentVerse (Chen et al. 2023/ICLR 2024)
AgentVerse dynamically composes an expert-agent group via a four-stage recruit/decide/act/evaluate loop, achieving a 'greater-than-the-sum-of-its-parts' system that outperforms a single agent and shows emergent social behaviors.
M051ICLR 2024
DyLAN (Liu et al. 2023)
DyLAN is a Dynamic LLM-Powered Agent Network that selects a task-specific team using an unsupervised Agent Importance Score and lets agents collaborate dynamically, deactivating weak contributors.
M053COLM 2024
Magentic-One (Fourney et al./Microsoft 2024)
Magentic-One is a generalist manager-worker multi-agent system where an Orchestrator with task/progress ledgers directs specialized Coder, Executor, FileSurfer, and WebSurfer agents, reaching competitive results on GAIA, AssistantBench, and WebArena.
M054arXiv 2024 (Microsoft; built on AutoGen)
OpenAI Swarm / Agents SDK (2024-2025)
OpenAI Swarm is a minimal orchestration library built on two primitives—routines (an agent = instructions + tools) and handoffs (a function that returns another agent)—later superseded by the production Agents SDK.
M055OpenAI (GitHub, Oct 2024) / Agents SDK (Mar 2025)
AutoGen (Wu et al. 2023)
AutoGen is an open-source framework of customizable, conversable agents that solve tasks by messaging one another, mixing LLMs, tools, and human input.
M010arXiv 2023 (later COLM 2024 workshop / widely cited)
CAMEL (Li et al. 2023)
CAMEL uses role-playing with 'inception prompting' to let two communicative agents autonomously cooperate on a task, also generating conversational data for studying agent behavior.
M012NeurIPS 2023
LangGraph vs CrewAI (framework references)
Two dominant production orchestration idioms: LangGraph models agents as an explicit state-machine graph (typed shared state, checkpointing, cycles), while CrewAI models top-down role-based crews (sequential or hierarchical processes).
